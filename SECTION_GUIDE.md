## PLACEMENT RULES

1. If a topic spans two sections -> place in the section where its **PRIMARY evidence type** lives. Cross-reference the other.
2. Never duplicate full content across sections -> use cross-references instead.
3. **H section is meta-only** -> analysis ABOUT the research, not research itself.
4. Modern science topics go in **G** if they CONNECT to ancient knowledge. Pure science goes in **Q**, **R**, **ZA**, or **ZB**.
5. When in doubt, ask: "What TYPE of evidence is this primarily about?" That's your section.
6. **C vs W:** C is for mythology, comparative analysis, and cross-cultural patterns. W is for civilization profiles, religions, and cultural systems.
7. **K vs Y:** K is about consciousness THEORY (hard problem, neural correlates, IIT). Y is about altered STATES (psychedelics, NDEs, meditation, shamanic).
8. **L vs Z:** L is about evolutionary genetics and human origins. Z is about molecular mechanisms (gene expression, CRISPR, genomics).
9. **Q vs ZA:** Q is about cosmology and astrophysics (large-scale universe). ZA is about fundamental physics (quantum mechanics, particles, forces).
10. **R vs ZB:** R is about evolution and life origins. ZB is about ecology, organisms, and biological systems.
11. **T vs ZC:** T is about individual psychology. ZC is about social-level psychology and anthropology.
12. **V vs ZD:** V is about pure mathematics. ZD is about information theory, computation, and applied math/CS.
13. **P vs ZE:** P is about theoretical philosophy. ZE is about applied ethics, political philosophy, and ritual/symbol.
14. **O vs ZF:** O is about anomalous geophysical phenomena (ley lines, Bermuda Triangle, Skinwalker). ZF is about ocean science and maritime knowledge (thermohaline, coral reefs, deep-sea ecosystems, tides, marine archaeology, USOs).
15. **X vs J:** X is about medicine, healing, and pharmacology. J is about ancient engineering and technology (power systems, vimanas, materials).
