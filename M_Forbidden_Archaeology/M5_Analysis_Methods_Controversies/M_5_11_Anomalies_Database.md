## QUICK SUMMARY

This document serves as a **structured database and classification system** for archaeological anomalies — finds that appear to challenge accepted timelines, technological capabilities, or historical frameworks. Rather than advocating for or against any particular interpretation, this reference catalogs anomalous claims by **category**, **evidence quality**, **current status** (resolved, debated, unresolved), and **bibliography**. Drawing on the cataloging traditions of **Charles Fort** (1919-1932), **William Corliss** (1974-2009, *Sourcebook Project*), and **Michael Cremo** (1993, *Forbidden Archeology*), this database applies a consistent evaluation framework based on **provenance documentation**, **independent verification**, **dating reliability**, and **reproducibility**. The database covers eight anomaly categories: (1) Out-of-place artifacts (OOPArts); (2) Anomalous human remains in deep strata; (3) Anomalous engineering or precision; (4) Anomalous dating results; (5) Unexplained structures; (6) Anomalous maps and navigation; (7) Anomalous biological/genetic finds; and (8) Anomalous geological inclusions. Each category is populated with representative cases, their current scientific status, and references to detailed treatment elsewhere in this archive.

### 2.3 Evaluation Framework
- For any anomalous archaeological claim, five criteria determine evidential weight:
  1. **Provenance**: Is the find spot documented by trained excavators with photographs, section drawings, and context records? (Critical — without provenance, no evaluation is possible)
  2. **Independent verification**: Has the find been examined by multiple independent specialists? Single-investigator claims are inherently weaker
  3. **Dating reliability**: Is the date based on multiple independent dating methods applied to the artifact itself (not just associated materials)?
  4. **Alternative explanations**: Have conventional explanations (natural formation, contamination, disturbance, hoax) been systematically ruled out?
  5. **Theoretical magnitude**: Does the claim require minor adjustment of existing models (likely) or wholesale rejection of well-established frameworks (extraordinary evidence required)?

## 4. DUBIOUS CLAIMS (Tier 4 — No Credible Source / Contradicted by Evidence)

### 4.1 All Anomalies Are Evidence of a Lost Advanced Civilization
- **[OVERSTATED]** While some anomalies are genuine and have forced paradigm revisions, the vast majority have conventional explanations. Pattern-seeking interpretation of unrelated anomalies into a unified "lost civilization" narrative requires evidence that does not exist

### 4.2 Science Systematically Suppresses All Anomalous Finds
- **[OVERSTATED]** Publication bias exists, but the track record shows that well-evidenced anomalies (pre-Clovis, Göbekli Tepe, Denisovans, Homo floresiensis) are accepted when they meet evidential standards. The "suppression" narrative confuses legitimate scientific skepticism with deliberate censorship

### 4.3 Ancient Alien Visitation Explains All Anomalies
- **[NOT SUPPORTED]** No authenticated archaeological artifact requires a non-human manufacturer. All verified anomalies have been explained within human technological capabilities or resolved as hoaxes/misidentifications
