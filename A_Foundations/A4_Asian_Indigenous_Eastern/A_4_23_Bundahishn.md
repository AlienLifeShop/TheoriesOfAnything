## QUICK SUMMARY

The **Bundahishn** (*Bundahišn*, "Primal Creation") is the most important **Zoroastrian cosmogonical text**, composed in **Middle Persian (Pahlavi)** during the 9th century CE but preserving traditions that are centuries or millennia older, rooted in the lost Avestan *Dāmdād Nask*. It exists in two recensions: the **Iranian/Greater Bundahishn** (36 chapters, more complete) and the **Indian Bundahishn** (34 chapters, preserved by the Parsi community). The text narrates the entire history of the cosmos from **creation through final renovation**: Ahura Mazda (Ohrmazd) creates the spiritual (*mēnōg*) and material (*gētīg*) worlds in a sequence of seven creations — sky, water, earth, plants, animals (the Primal Bull), humans (Gayōmard, the First Man), and fire. The evil spirit **Angra Mainyu** (Ahriman) attacks creation, introducing death, disease, pollution, and noxious creatures. The text then describes **cosmography** (the earth's structure, mountains, seas, rivers), **geography**, **genealogy** of Iranian rulers, **astral mythology** (planets as demons, constellations as warriors), and **eschatology** — the final renovation (*Frashōkereti*) when evil is permanently defeated. The Bundahishn is indispensable for understanding Zoroastrian cosmology, its influence on Jewish-Christian-Islamic apocalypticism, and the ancient Iranian worldview of cosmic dualism.

## 1. VERIFIED CLAIMS (Tier 1 — Peer-Reviewed / Archaeological Record)

### 1.1 Manuscript Tradition and Date
- The Bundahishn is written in **Middle Persian** (Pahlavi script) — the literary language of the Sasanian Empire (224–651 CE) and the Zoroastrian priestly class
- **Greater/Iranian Bundahishn** (GBd): Preserved in manuscript TD2 (copied 1531 CE in Iran) — 36 chapters, more complete
- **Indian Bundahishn** (IBd): Preserved in manuscripts of the Parsi (Indian Zoroastrian) community — 34 chapters, somewhat abbreviated
- **Composition date**: 9th century CE during the early Islamic period, when Zoroastrian priests compiled and systematized Avestan theological traditions into Pahlavi encyclopedic works
- The text explicitly states it derives from the **Dāmdād Nask**, one of the 21 *nasks* (books) of the Sasanian-era Avesta, now entirely lost — thus preserving traditions far older than its composition date

### 1.2 The Seven Creations
- Ahura Mazda (Ohrmazd) creates the universe in **seven stages**, each producing a fundamental element of the good creation:
  1. **Sky** (*āsmān*): A hard shell of luminous stone enclosing the cosmos
  2. **Water** (*āb*): Filling the lower part of the cosmic shell
  3. **Earth** (*zamīg*): Floating on the cosmic waters
  4. **Plants** (*urwar*): The Primal Plant from which all vegetation derives
  5. **Animals** (*gōspand*): The uniquely created **Primal Bull** (*Gāw ī Ēwdād*) — from whose slain body all animal species arise
  6. **Humans** (*mardōm*): **Gayōmard** (Middle Persian *Gayōmard*, Avestan *Gaya Marətan*, "Mortal Life") — the First Man
  7. **Fire** (*ātaxš*): Pervading all creation, visible in hearth fires and in the life-force of all beings
- Each creation has a spiritual prototype (*mēnōg*) created first, then a material (*gētīg*) manifestation

### 1.3 The Assault of Evil
- Angra Mainyu (Ahriman) attacks the good creation:
  - Pierces the sky shell, entering the material world
  - Pollutes the waters, scorches the earth, withers the Primal Plant
  - **Kills the Primal Bull** — from its body spring all animal species; from its seed, purified by the moon, come all beneficial animals
  - **Kills Gayōmard** — from his body spring metals (gold from his flesh); from his seed, purified by the sun, grow the first human couple: **Mashya** and **Mashyānag**
  - Creates **noxious creatures** (*xrafstar*): wolves, snakes, scorpions, flies — each embodying a specific form of evil

### 1.4 Cosmography
- The earth consists of **seven regions** (*kišwar*), with **Xwaniratha** (the central and largest region) as the inhabited world
- **Mount Harā** (Avestan *Harā Bərəzaitī* = Alborz) encircles the earth — the sun, moon, and stars revolve around it
- The cosmic ocean **Vourukasha** and the rivers **Veh Rōd** and **Arang Rōd** define the earth's geography
- Stars are organized as warriors against demonic planets — the **constellations** serve as generals in the cosmic army

## 2. CREDIBLE CLAIMS (Tier 2 — Academic / Debated but Supported)

### 2.1 Relationship to the Lost Avesta
- The Bundahishn explicitly cites the *Dāmdād Nask* and other lost Avestan texts — scholars generally accept it preserves **genuine pre-Islamic Iranian traditions**, though filtered through 9th-century editorial work (Anklesaria 1956; Bailey 1971)
- Some cosmological elements (seven creations, Primal Bull, Gayōmard) correspond to passages in the surviving **Avesta** (Yasna, Yashts, Vendidad) — confirming antiquity

### 2.2 Cosmic Dualism and Historical Framework
- The text presents a **12,000-year cosmic history** divided into four periods of 3,000 years each:
  1. Spiritual creation (Ohrmazd's preparatory period)
  2. Material creation (peaceful period before Ahriman's attack)
  3. Mixture (*gumēzišn*): The current age — good and evil are mixed in the material world
  4. Separation (*wizārišn*): The final renovation, defeat of evil
- This systematic dualist chronology influenced **Jewish apocalypticism** (particularly the periodization in Daniel and the Dead Sea Scrolls) and later Christian and Islamic eschatological chronologies (Boyce 1975; Shaked 1994)

### 2.3 Astral Mythology
- **Planets** (*axtarān*) are treated as **demons** — agents of Ahriman attacking the celestial order:
  - Jupiter (*Ohrmazd*): An exception, associated with Ahura Mazda
  - Saturn, Mars, Venus, Mercury: Demonic forces
- **Constellations** are divine warriors: Tishtrya (Sirius) battles the demon of drought (Apaosha)
- This astral demonology influenced Hellenistic and Gnostic systems associating planetary rulers with hostile *archons* (Cumont 1912; Panaino 1990)

## 3. SPECULATIVE CLAIMS (Tier 3 — Possible but Unverified)

### 3.1 Indo-Iranian Antiquity
- Some elements — the Primal Bull sacrifice, the cosmic mountain, the world-encircling ocean — may derive from **Indo-Iranian** (pre-Zoroastrian) mythology, with parallels in Vedic cosmogony (the sacrifice of Purusha in Rigveda 10.90)
- The depth of this connection and whether it represents common inheritance or convergent evolution is debated

### 3.2 Influence on Genesis
- The seven creations parallel (but do not exactly match) the **six days of creation in Genesis** — scholars have proposed Zoroastrian influence on the Priestly creation account composed during or after Babylonian exile
- Direct textual dependence is undemonstrable, though general cultural contact during the Persian period is well established

## 4. DUBIOUS CLAIMS (Tier 4 — No Credible Source / Contradicted by Evidence)

### 4.1 Direct Zarathustra Authorship
- **[LEGENDARY]** Though the traditions claim ultimate derivation from Zarathustra's revelation, the Bundahishn is a 9th-century compilation — Zarathustra's historical date (variously placed 1700–600 BCE) precludes him as author of a Middle Persian text
