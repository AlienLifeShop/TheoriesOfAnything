## QUICK SUMMARY

The **Wilson-Davis Memo** (also called the "Wilson Notes" or "Wilson-Davis Notes") refers to a set of **notes allegedly taken by physicist Dr. Eric W. Davis** documenting a meeting on **October 16, 2002**, with **Vice Admiral Thomas R. Wilson** (USN, Ret.) — who served as Director of the Defense Intelligence Agency (DIA) from 1999 to 2002 and previously as J-2 (Director of Intelligence) of the Joint Chiefs of Staff (1997–1999). According to these notes (which circulated first in the UAP research community and were published online by journalist Richard Dolan in 2019 after they were leaked from the estate of astronaut **Edgar Mitchell** following his death in 2016), Admiral Wilson described his attempt to uncover classified programs related to the **reverse engineering of recovered non-human technology** — and his assertion that he was **denied access** to a highly compartmented Special Access Program (SAP) operated by a **defense contractor**, despite his status as the third-highest intelligence official in the Department of Defense. The notes describe the following narrative: in **1997**, while serving as J-2, Wilson was briefed by individuals (prompted by a meeting involving Dr. Steven Greer and Edgar Mitchell at the Pentagon on April 9, 1997, which itself is documented) on the existence of a special program allegedly possessing materials of non-human origin; Wilson then attempted to identify and access this program through the Special Access Program Oversight Committee (SAPOC); he located what he described as a tightly controlled **unacknowledged waived SAP** operated by a major defense contractor; when he attempted to gain access, the program's managers (identified only as "gatekeepers" — a program director, a corporate attorney, and a security officer) denied him entry, telling him the program dealt with a "technology that was not of this Earth — not made by man — not by human hands" and that it had achieved very little progress in understanding or replicating the materials; Wilson was told to drop the matter, and when he protested, he was threatened by senior Pentagon officials who indicated that pursuit would damage his career. The memo's **authenticity is fiercely debated**: proponents argue that (a) the level of detail and procedural knowledge in the notes (SAP oversight mechanisms, OUSDI organizational structures, promotion politics) is consistent with insider knowledge, (b) Eric Davis has not categorically denied the meeting (he confirmed its authenticity in a 2019 radio interview according to some interpretations, though he later declined comment), and (c) Admiral Wilson has been conspicuously restrained in his denials (he has stated he does not recall such a conversation and that the notes contain inaccuracies, but has not filed legal action or made forceful public denials). Skeptics argue that (a) the notes are typed (not handwritten, and therefore more easily fabricated), (b) no corroborating classified documentation has surfaced, (c) it is implausible that a DIA Director would discuss such sensitive matters in a private conversation with a non-cleared physicist, and (d) the notes may reflect genuine conversations embellished or misinterpreted over time. The Wilson-Davis Memo gained renewed significance following **David Grusch's 2023 Congressional testimony** that the U.S. government possesses materials and "biologics" of non-human origin — Grusch has stated that the Wilson Notes are broadly consistent with what he was told during his time as a National Reconnaissance Office representative.

## 1. VERIFIED CLAIMS (Tier 1 — Documented Facts)

### 1.1 Identities and Positions
- **Vice Admiral Thomas R. Wilson**: confirmed positions — J-2 of the Joint Chiefs of Staff (1997–1999), Director of DIA (1999–2002), retired 2002; these positions would have given him access to the highest levels of U.S. intelligence, including oversight of Special Access Programs
- **Dr. Eric W. Davis**: confirmed physicist — worked at the National Institute for Discovery Science (NIDS, Robert Bigelow's research organization), later EarthTech International and the Institute for Advanced Studies at Austin; contributed Defense Intelligence Reference Documents (DIRDs) to the AAWSAP program; holds security clearances
- **The Greer-Mitchell-Wilson Pentagon meeting**: a meeting on **April 9, 1997**, involving Dr. Steven Greer, Dr. Edgar Mitchell, and military/intelligence officials (including, by some accounts, Admiral Wilson as J-2) at the Pentagon is documented by multiple participants; what was discussed and what follow-up occurred is disputed

### 1.2 SAP Oversight Structure
- The notes describe SAP oversight mechanisms — **SAPOC (Special Access Program Oversight Committee)**, **waived vs. unwaived SAPs**, **carve-outs**, and contractor-managed programs — in ways that are procedurally accurate according to former government officials and published descriptions of the SAP system (e.g., DoD Manual 5205.07)
- The concept of an **"unacknowledged waived SAP"** — a program so sensitive that even its existence is classified, and standard Congressional oversight committees are not briefed — is a real category in the U.S. classification system, though such programs are rare

## 2. CREDIBLE CLAIMS (Tier 2 — Supporting Circumstantial Evidence)

### 2.1 Document Provenance
- The notes were found among the papers of **Edgar Mitchell** (Apollo 14 astronaut, 1930–2016; longtime UAP disclosure advocate) after his death — Mitchell was a known associate of both Davis and Greer and had publicly advocated for UAP disclosure for decades
- **Richard Dolan** published the notes in June 2019, stating they had been authenticated by "multiple knowledgeable sources" — however, no independent forensic document analysis has been published

### 2.2 Grusch Corroboration
- **David Grusch** (former UAPTF/NRO intelligence officer): in his 2023 Congressional testimony and media interviews, Grusch stated that the U.S. government possesses "intact and partially intact vehicles of non-human origin" and associated "biologics" — while not directly referencing the Wilson-Davis Memo in testimony, Grusch has been reported by journalists as considering the notes consistent with his own findings
- The convergence of the Wilson-Davis narrative with Grusch's Congressional testimony and the NDAA FY2024 provision for a **UAP Records Review Board** (modeled on the JFK Assassination Records Act) is cited by proponents as institutional corroboration

## 3. SPECULATIVE CLAIMS (Tier 3 — Possible but Unverified)

### 3.1 Reverse Engineering Program Existence
- The core claim — that a defense contractor operates a classified program possessing and attempting to reverse-engineer materials of non-human origin — is the central unverified assertion; no physical evidence, program documentation, or confirmed whistleblower testimony from within the alleged program has surfaced publicly
- Whether such a program could have been maintained in secrecy across multiple decades (since at least the late 1940s–1950s, if the memo's implied timeline is accurate) is itself debated — it would require an extraordinary level of compartmentalization and a willingness by successive administrations and Congressional members to accept exclusion from oversight
