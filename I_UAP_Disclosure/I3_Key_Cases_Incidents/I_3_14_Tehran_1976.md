## QUICK SUMMARY

On **September 19, 1976**, Iranian Air Force **F-4 Phantom II** jets were scrambled from Shahrokhi Air Base (now Hamadan Air Base) to intercept a brilliant unidentified object reported over Tehran. The incident — documented in a **U.S. Defense Intelligence Agency (DIA)** evaluation, Iranian Air Force records, and pilot testimony — is considered one of the best-documented military-UAP encounters in history — analyzed in detail by **Leslie Kean** in *UFOs: Generals, Pilots, and Government Officials Go on the Record* (Harmony Books, 2010, ISBN: 9781441776198) — because it involves: **(1) multiple independent radar and visual confirmations**, **(2) attempted weapons engagement** (with reported systems failure at the moment of weapons lock), **(3) electromagnetic effects** on aircraft instruments, **(4) official documentation** by both Iranian and U.S. military intelligence, and **(5) credible military witnesses** including experienced jet fighter pilots. The DIA's evaluation of the event rated it as meeting all criteria for a valid intelligence report. General **Parviz Jafari**, the pilot of the second F-4, has given extensive public testimony about the encounter — describing an object that outmaneuvered his aircraft, jammed his weapons systems when he attempted to fire an AIM-9 missile, and released a smaller luminous object that approached his aircraft before returning to the parent object.

## 1. VERIFIED CLAIMS (Tier 1 — Peer-Reviewed / Archaeological Record)

### 1.1 The Event Sequence
- The incident unfolded in stages documented by official records:
  - **Initial reports** (~10:30 PM local time): citizens in northern Tehran reported a large, brilliant, multicolored light visible in the night sky. Calls to Mehrabad Airport and the Imperial Iranian Air Force (IIAF) control tower
  - **First intercept**: an F-4 Phantom II was scrambled from Shahrokhi Air Base. The pilot reported visual contact with the object, which appeared as an intensely bright light. As the F-4 approached within approximately 25 nautical miles, the pilot reported **instrument failures** — communications and navigation systems malfunctioned. He broke off the intercept and turned back toward base; instruments returned to normal once he moved away from the object
  - **Second intercept**: a second F-4 was scrambled, piloted by **Lieutenant (later General) Parviz Jafari**. Jafari acquired the object on radar at 27 nautical miles — the return was described as comparable to a Boeing 707 tanker. The object was also visually observed as emitting intense multicolored light (red, green, blue/white, alternating rapidly)
  - **Weapons attempt**: as Jafari closed on the object, he reported a smaller luminous object detaching from the primary object and accelerating directly toward his aircraft. He attempted to fire an **AIM-9 Sidewinder** missile — at that instant, his weapons control panel and communications systems went dead. He executed a sharp evasive turn; the smaller object followed briefly then returned to the primary object
  - **Additional objects**: a second smaller luminous object detached and descended rapidly toward the ground below. Jafari and his weapons officer observed it illuminate a wide area of terrain before appearing to land gently or hover near the surface

### 1.2 Official Documentation
- The incident was documented in an official **DIA evaluation report** (document reference DIA-HQ-76-0927, released through FOIA):
  - The DIA report rated the case as meeting **all criteria for a valid intelligence report**: the source was reliable, the information was confirmed by multiple means, and the evaluation concluded the report was "outstanding"
  - The DIA document summarized: multiple visual sightings from ground and air, radar contact by at least two aircraft, electromagnetic effects on two separate interceptor aircraft, and attempted weapons engagement
  - The document was distributed to the White House, Secretary of State, Joint Chiefs of Staff, National Security Agency, and CIA — indicating the event was taken seriously at the highest levels of the U.S. intelligence community

### 1.3 Iranian Air Force Confirmation
- The Imperial Iranian Air Force officially acknowledged the incident:
  - The scramble orders, pilot debriefings, and radar tracking records were generated through standard military channels
  - General **Nader Yousefi**, the deputy commander of operations at the IIAF Command Post in Tehran who ordered the second scramble, confirmed the events
  - The incident was discussed in Iranian military circles as a genuinely anomalous event that defied conventional explanation

## 2. CREDIBLE CLAIMS (Tier 2 — Academic / Debated but Supported)

### 2.1 Pilot Testimony
- General **Parviz Jafari** (retired) has given extensive public testimony about the encounter:
  - Testified at the National Press Club on November 12, 2007, alongside other international military pilots
  - Described the object as performing maneuvers that exceeded any known aircraft capability — maintaining distance despite his F-4's maximum speed, maneuvering instantaneously, and releasing and reabsorbing smaller objects
  - The weapons system failure at the precise moment of attempted engagement — and the automatic restoration of systems after breaking off — suggests a targeted electromagnetic effect rather than random malfunction
  - Jafari has maintained his account consistently for decades and is considered a highly credible witness — a career military officer with extensive tactical aviation experience

### 2.2 Electromagnetic Weapon Implications
- The reported electromagnetic effects on both F-4s raise significant implications:
  - If the reports are accurate, they describe a capability to selectively disable specific aircraft systems (weapons, communications) at range — technology far beyond any known 1976 capabilities
  - The restoration of systems after moving away from the object argues against permanent damage — consistent with an active electromagnetic suppression field rather than a destructive pulse
  - Similar electromagnetic effects have been reported in other military-UAP encounters (Malmstrom missile shutdowns, USS Princeton/Nimitz Tic Tac electronics interference), as catalogued by **Richard F. Haines** in NARCAP Technical Report 7 (2001), which analyzed 64 pilot sighting reports involving electromagnetic effects on aircraft systems

### 2.3 Conventional Explanations Assessed
- Proposed conventional explanations include Jupiter (which was bright and visible that night), satellite re-entry, or aircraft misidentification:
  - Jupiter explains a bright stationary light but does not account for radar returns, object movement, electromagnetic effects on two separate aircraft, or the release of smaller objects
  - No satellite re-entry was recorded for that date and location
  - The object was pursued by F-4 Phantoms piloted by experienced military aviators who could distinguish aircraft and celestial objects

## 4. DUBIOUS CLAIMS (Tier 4 — No Credible Source / Contradicted by Evidence)

### 4.1 The Event Was a U.S. Black Project Test
- **[CONTRADICTED]** The electromagnetic effects on U.S.-supplied F-4 aircraft, the DIA's expression of genuine intelligence interest, and the distribution to the highest levels of the U.S. government argue strongly against this being a known U.S. technology demonstration — the U.S. would not test secret weapons against an allied nation's air force without that nation's knowledge

### 4.2 The Pilots Were Confused by Jupiter
- **[INSUFFICIENT]** While Jupiter was visible, it does not produce radar returns, emit multicolored lights, release smaller objects, or disable weapons systems. The Jupiter hypothesis addresses only one data point while ignoring the majority of the evidence
