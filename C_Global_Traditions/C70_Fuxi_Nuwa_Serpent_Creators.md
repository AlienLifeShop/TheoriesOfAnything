## QUICK SUMMARY

**Fuxi** (伏羲) and **Nüwa** (女媧) are the primordial creator deities of Chinese mythology — typically depicted with **human upper bodies and intertwined serpent tails**, representing the foundational pair from whom all humanity descends. Fuxi is credited with inventing the **Eight Trigrams** (八卦, *bāguà*) that underlie the *Yijing* (I Ching), establishing hunting, fishing, animal domestication, and marriage customs. Nüwa **created humans** from yellow earth/clay, **repaired the sky** after a cosmic catastrophe (the tilting of heaven), and is often identified as the savior of humanity from a great **flood**. Their intertwined serpent-bodied iconography — especially prominent in **Han dynasty** tomb art (206 BCE – 220 CE) — bears a striking visual resemblance to the Mesopotamian intertwined serpents of **Ningishzida** (→ [B32](../B_Beings_and_Entities/B32_Ningishzida_Serpent_Deity.md)) and the later **caduceus**, though no direct transmission has been demonstrated. First textual evidence: the **Chu Silk Manuscript** (~300 BCE, the oldest Chinese manuscript on silk) names Fuxi as a cosmogonic figure; Nüwa appears in *Chu Ci* (*Songs of Chu*, ~300 BCE). Their combined mythology addresses creation, cosmic repair, flood survival, and the origins of human civilization.

## §1 — PRIMARY TEXT EVIDENCE

### Earliest Sources

| Source | Date | Content |
|--------|------|---------|
| **Chu Silk Manuscript** (楚帛書) | ~300 BCE (Warring States) | Fuxi (*Baoxi* 包犧) as cosmogonic figure who ordered heaven and earth; earliest known mention | 
| **Chu Ci** (*Songs of Chu*) | ~300 BCE | "Nüwa had a body — who formed and fashioned it?" (Tian Wen/Heavenly Questions) — implies Nüwa as creator but questions who created her |
| **Shan Hai Jing** (*Classic of Mountains and Seas*) | ~3rd–1st c. BCE | Nüwa's intestines become ten spirits; registers her as a divine being among many |
| **Huainanzi** (淮南子) | 139 BCE | The most detailed Nüwa creation narrative (Chapter 6): Nüwa smelts five-colored stones to repair the sky, cuts off the legs of a giant turtle to support heaven, kills the black dragon, accumulates reed ashes to stop the flooding waters |
| **Fengsutongyi** (*Comprehensive Meanings of Customs*) | ~2nd c. CE | Brother-sister marriage after flood: Fuxi and Nüwa are the only survivors; they marry to repopulate humanity |
| **Duyizhi** and later compilations | 3rd–6th c. CE | Nüwa creates humans from yellow clay; nobles from hand-shaped figures, commoners from figures made by dragging a rope through mud |

- **Key observation:** Fuxi and Nüwa are **not originally paired** — Fuxi appears in the earliest sources as a solitary culture hero, and Nüwa appears independently as a creator/repairer. Their **combination as a married pair** is a later development, becoming standard by the **Han dynasty**
- **Tier 1** — All texts published and translated; Birrell (1993), Lewis (2006)

## §2 — FUXI: THE CULTURE HERO

### Inventions and Contributions

According to various sources (*Xici Zhuan* appendix of the *Yijing*, *Baihu Tong*, *Shiji* traditions):

| Attribution | Description | Significance |
|-------------|-------------|-------------|
| **Eight Trigrams** (八卦, bāguà) | Observed patterns in heaven and earth; inscribed the trigrams that form the basis of the *Yijing* | Foundation of Chinese cosmological thought |
| **Knotted cords** | Invented rope-knotting for record-keeping (before writing) | Proto-information technology |
| **Nets** | Taught humans fishing and hunting with nets | Subsistence technology |
| **Animal domestication** | Taught animal husbandry | Agriculture/pastoralism |
| **Marriage customs** | Established marriage rites and social order | Civilizational order |
| **Music** | Invented the *sè* (瑟, zither) and composed music | Cultural arts |
| **Calendar** | Ordered the seasons | Temporal order |

- Fuxi as **culture hero** exactly parallels figures like Prometheus (fire), Thoth (writing), Oannes (civilization from the sea), and Quetzalcoatl (maize, calendar) — the "divine teacher" archetype
- His title **Taihao** (太昊, "Great Brilliance") and his position as the **first of the Three Sovereigns** (三皇) place him at the absolute beginning of Chinese legendary history
- **Tier 1–2** — Textual attributions are documented; historicity is legendary

### The Eight Trigrams

- The *Xici Zhuan* (Great Commentary on the *Yijing*): "In ancient times, when Baoxi ruled the world, he looked up and contemplated the images in the heavens; he looked down and contemplated the patterns on the earth...and thus he made the eight trigrams"
- Each trigram is three lines (broken = yin, unbroken = yang) representing fundamental cosmic categories:
  - ☰ Qián (Heaven) · ☷ Kūn (Earth) · ☲ Lí (Fire) · ☵ Kǎn (Water) · ☳ Zhèn (Thunder) · ☴ Xùn (Wind) · ☶ Gèn (Mountain) · ☱ Duì (Lake)
- Fuxi's trigrams = the **binary language** of Chinese cosmology, forming 64 hexagrams that map all possible states of change
- **Tier 1** — The *Yijing* and its commentaries are among the most studied texts in world literature

## §3 — NÜWA: THE CREATOR AND REPAIRER

### Creation of Humans

- **Fengsutongyi** and later texts: Nüwa creates humans from **yellow earth** (黃土):
  - First batch: hand-shaped individually → become the **nobles and wealthy**
  - Later, tired, she drags a **rope** through the mud and the droplets become the **common people**
- This etiological myth explains social hierarchy — an explanation pattern also found in Mesopotamian traditions (Enki creating humans from clay to serve the gods) and the Bible (God forming Adam from *adamah*, "earth/clay")
- **Tier 2** — Texts are documented; social-hierarchy etiology is the standard scholarly reading

### Repair of the Sky

- **Huainanzi** 6 — the most detailed account:
  - The four pillars supporting the sky collapsed; the nine provinces (*jiuzhou*) cracked; heaven did not cover all, earth did not support all; fires raged, floods surged, wild animals devoured the people
  - **Nüwa's response** (four remedial acts):
    1. **Smelted five-colored stones** to patch the broken sky
    2. **Cut off the legs of a giant turtle** (*ao* 鰲) to serve as four pillars
    3. **Killed the black dragon** to save the land of Ji (central China)
    4. **Accumulated reed ashes** to stop the flooding waters
  - After repair: "Heaven was rounded and earth was squared" — cosmic order restored
- This narrative is a **cosmological repair myth** — the world suffers a structural catastrophe and a divine being restores it. The tilted sky (northwest high, southeast low — explaining why Chinese rivers flow east) is an etiological element
- **Tier 1** — Primary text published and translated (Major et al. 2010)

### Flood and Repopulation

- In folk traditions collected from the 2nd century CE onward (especially in southern China and among ethnic minorities — Miao, Yao, Zhuang):
  - A great **flood** destroys humanity
  - Only **Fuxi and Nüwa** (brother and sister) survive, sheltered inside a gourd
  - They debate whether to marry (since they are siblings); they perform divination — rolling stones down a mountain (if the stones come together = heaven approves)
  - The stones meet; they marry and repopulate the earth
  - Nüwa gives birth to a lump of flesh; they cut it into pieces and scatter them → pieces become humans
- **Comparison with global flood myths:** (→ [E01](../E_Cataclysms_and_Chronology/E01_Younger_Dryas_Impact.md)) — structural parallels with Utnapishtim, Noah, Deucalion/Pyrrha (Greek — who also repopulate by throwing stones), and Manu (Hindu). The brother-sister marriage and gourd motif are specific to the Southeast/East Asian flood myth complex
- **Tier 2** — Documented in ethnographic collections; the antiquity of the tradition is debated

## §4 — INTERTWINED SERPENT ICONOGRAPHY

### Visual Evidence

- **Han dynasty tomb art** (206 BCE – 220 CE) provides the most iconic depictions: Fuxi and Nüwa with **human upper bodies** and **serpent/dragon tails intertwined below**, each holding implements:
  - **Fuxi** holds a **carpenter's square** (矩, *jǔ*) — symbolizing earth, measurement, order
  - **Nüwa** holds a **compass** (規, *guī*) — symbolizing heaven, circularity, cosmic rotation
  - Between them: often the **sun** (with a three-legged crow inside) and **moon** (with a toad or rabbit)
- Key archaeological examples:
  - **Astana Cemetery, Turfan** (Xinjiang, ~7th c. CE): Painted silk banners of Fuxi-Nüwa with intertwined tails — placed on the chest of the deceased. Over 30 examples excavated
  - **Wuliang Shrine** (Shandong, ~151 CE): Stone relief carvings of Fuxi-Nüwa with intertwined tails
  - **Mawangdui** (Hunan, ~168 BCE): Silk painting (T-shaped banner) with intertwining serpentine figures interpreted by some as Fuxi-Nüwa
- **Tier 1** — Archaeological objects are published, many in museum collections worldwide

### Comparison with Mesopotamian Intertwined Serpents

| Chinese | Mesopotamian | Assessment |
|---------|-------------|-----------|
| Fuxi + Nüwa: human torsos, serpent tails intertwined | Ningishzida: two serpents intertwined on a central axis (Gudea Libation Vase, ~2100 BCE) | Visual similarity: strong |
| Compass + square = heaven + earth | Caduceus: later Greek version of intertwined serpents | Functional similarity: both associated with cosmic order |
| Dating: Han dynasty depictions (~200 BCE – 200 CE) | Dating: Sumerian originals (~2100 BCE) | Mesopotamian predates Chinese by ~1900 years |
| Possible transmission route: Silk Road through Central Asia? | No material evidence of direct transmission documented | **Tier 3** — The visual parallel is striking but no causal link has been demonstrated |

- **Scholarly assessment:** The similarity has been noted by scholars (Rawson 2017; Wu Hung 1989) but **no direct transmission mechanism** has been identified. Possibilities include: (a) diffusion via Silk Road contacts, (b) convergent symbolism (two intertwined forms = cosmic duality), or (c) deep shared mythological substrate. Without material evidence of transmission, this remains speculative
- **Tier 3** for the diffusion claim; **Tier 1** for each tradition independently

## §5 — NÜWA AND FUXI IN BROADER CHINESE THOUGHT

### Cosmological Significance

- The Fuxi-Nüwa pair encodes a **yin-yang complementarity**:
  - Fuxi = yang (male, square, earth-ordering, cultural invention)
  - Nüwa = yin (female, compass, sky-repairing, biological creation)
  - Their intertwined tails = the union of complementary opposites — the visual ancestor of the taijitu (yin-yang symbol)
- **The Three Sovereigns tradition** (三皇, *sān huáng*) places Fuxi as the first legendary ruler of China, followed by Nüwa (in some lists) or Shennong (Divine Farmer). This positions them as the absolute beginning of Chinese civilization
- **Confucian reinterpretation:** Fuxi becomes the exemplary sage-king who orders the world through pattern recognition (trigrams); Nüwa becomes the nurturing mother who restores what is broken. The Confucian tradition emphasized Fuxi's rational ordering; the Daoist tradition emphasized Nüwa's creative spontaneity

## §6 — COUNTER-ARGUMENTS AND CRITICAL ASSESSMENT

### Against Literal Reading

- **Mythic Lens:** Fuxi and Nüwa are cosmogonic symbols — their serpent bodies represent the undifferentiated, primordial state from which differentiated human civilization emerges
- **Literal Lens:** No historical or archaeological evidence for serpent-bodied beings. Some euhemeristic interpretations suggest they were **tribal leaders** whose totemic animal was the serpent, later mythologized
- **Symbolic Lens:** The intertwined serpents represent the **cosmic marriage** of complementary principles (yin-yang) — the fundamental structure of Chinese cosmological thought

### Against Ancient Astronaut / DNA Claims

- **Claim:** "The intertwined serpents of Fuxi-Nüwa represent the DNA double helix" (various pseudoscience)
- **Assessment:** **Tier 4** — This is nearly identical to claims about Ningishzida's intertwined serpents (→ [B32](../B_Beings_and_Entities/B32_Ningishzida_Serpent_Deity.md)). The DNA structure was discovered in 1953; there is zero evidence any ancient culture possessed molecular biology knowledge. The intertwined form is far more naturally explained by (a) the mating posture of actual serpents, (b) the visual symbolism of two-becoming-one, and (c) cord/rope-twisting as a manufacturing technique observable by any premodern culture
