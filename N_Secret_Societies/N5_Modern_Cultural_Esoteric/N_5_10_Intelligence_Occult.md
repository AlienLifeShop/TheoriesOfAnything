## QUICK SUMMARY

The intersection of **intelligence agencies** and **occult or paranormal phenomena** is one of the most extensively documented — yet still controversial — chapters in 20th-century intelligence history. Declassified documents (primarily through **FOIA requests** and congressional investigations) have confirmed that the **CIA**, **DIA** (Defense Intelligence Agency), **US Army**, **British intelligence**, and **Soviet/KGB** programs all invested significant resources in investigating and operationalizing **psychic phenomena**, **altered states of consciousness**, and **techniques derived from esoteric traditions**. The most prominent documented programs include: **MKUltra** (CIA, 1953-1973) — the infamous mind control program involving LSD, hypnosis, sensory deprivation, and other techniques, partially inspired by rumored Soviet and Chinese "brainwashing" research and involving researchers with occult interests like **Andrija Puharich**; **Project Stargate** (DIA/US Army, 1972-1995) — the **remote viewing** program at **Stanford Research Institute** (SRI), which employed psychics including **Ingo Swann** and **Pat Price** to attempt military intelligence gathering through clairvoyance; and various **Soviet parapsychology programs** (1960s-1980s) studying psychokinesis, telepathy, and bioenergetics. While the intelligence value of these programs remains debated, their **existence is not in question** — they are documented by declassified government records, congressional hearings (Church Committee, 1975), and the participants' own testimony.

## 1. VERIFIED CLAIMS (Tier 1 — Peer-Reviewed / Established)

### 1.1 MKUltra (CIA, 1953-1973)
- **Project MKUltra** was the CIA's program for researching mind control and behavioral modification:
  - Authorized by **CIA Director Allen Dulles** in April 1953
  - Managed by **Sidney Gottlieb** (1918-1999), chief of the CIA's Technical Services Staff (TSS)
  - **149 sub-projects** spanning: LSD administration (often without subjects' knowledge or consent — including the notorious case of **Frank Olson**, a CIA scientist who died after being covertly dosed with LSD in 1953), hypnosis, sensory deprivation, electroshock, psychological coercion, and more
  - **Funding channels**: MKUltra was funded through front organizations and grants to universities, hospitals, and research institutions — including **McGill University** (where Dr. **Donald Ewen Cameron** conducted destructive "psychic driving" experiments on unwitting patients)
  - **Document destruction**: CIA Director **Richard Helms** ordered the destruction of MKUltra files in 1973 — however, approximately **20,000 pages** survived due to a filing error and were discovered in 1977
  - Congressional exposure: the **Church Committee** (1975) and a follow-up Senate hearing (1977) publicly documented MKUltra's activities

### 1.2 Project Stargate (Remote Viewing)
- **Project Stargate** was the umbrella designation for the US government's **remote viewing** (psychic espionage) program:
  - Initiated in 1972 at **Stanford Research Institute** (SRI) under physicists **Hal Puthoff** and **Russell Targ**
  - Key remote viewers included: **Ingo Swann** (who reportedly described the rings of Jupiter before the Pioneer 10 flyby confirmed them), **Pat Price** (who reportedly identified Soviet military facilities from coordinates alone), and **Joe McMoneagle** (US Army intelligence)
  - The program operated under various codenames: **SCANATE**, **GONDOLA WISH**, **GRILL FLAME**, **CENTER LANE**, **SUN STREAK**, and finally **STARGATE**
  - Transferred between the CIA and DIA multiple times; ultimately declassified and terminated in **1995** after an **AIR** (American Institutes for Research) evaluation concluded that remote viewing had not produced actionable intelligence — though the AIR statistician **Jessica Utts** found the statistical evidence for a real effect to be significant
  - Total expenditure: approximately **$20 million** over 23 years

### 1.3 Soviet/Russian Parapsychology Programs
- The **Soviet Union** and later **Russia** maintained parapsychology research programs:
  - **Leonid Vasiliev** (1891-1966): Leningrad physiologist who conducted telepathy experiments from the 1920s and published *Experiments in Mental Suggestion* (1962)
  - Research institutes in Leningrad, Moscow, Novosibirsk, and Alma-Ata investigated telepathy, psychokinesis, and "bioenergetics"
  - The CIA's interest in parapsychology was partly motivated by intelligence reports (some confirmed, some exaggerated) of Soviet advances in "psychotronic weapons" and psychic research
  - Post-Soviet declassifications have confirmed the existence of these programs, though their scale and results remain debated

## 2. CREDIBLE CLAIMS (Tier 2 — Academic / Debated but Supported)

### 2.1 Occult Connections Within MKUltra
- Several MKUltra-adjacent researchers had connections to occult or esoteric traditions:
  - **Andrija Puharich** (1918-1995): physician and parapsychology researcher who conducted experiments for the US Army at the Round Table Foundation (Glen Cove, Maine) in the 1950s — studying psychedelic mushrooms, hypnosis, and channeling
  - Puharich later became involved with **Uri Geller** and channeling experiments, claiming contact with extraterrestrial intelligences ("The Nine")
  - **John C. Lilly** (1915-2001): neuroscientist who developed the **sensory deprivation tank** (isolation tank) under military funding, later explored dolphin communication and ketamine experiences — his work bridged legitimate neuroscience and countercultural mysticism
  - The degree to which these occult interests were personal hobbies versus operationally relevant remains debated

### 2.2 British Intelligence and the Occult
- **British intelligence** had documented interactions with occultism:
  - **Aleister Crowley** (1875-1947): while the extent of Crowley's intelligence work is debated, evidence confirms he had contacts with British intelligence:
    - During WWI, Crowley was in New York, reportedly working for British intelligence by writing pro-German propaganda so absurd it undermined the German cause (this interpretation is contested)
    - During WWII, **Ian Fleming** (naval intelligence officer, later creator of James Bond) reportedly proposed using Crowley to interrogate Rudolf Hess — though this plan was apparently not implemented
  - **Dennis Wheatley** (1897-1977): occult novelist who served in the Joint Planning Staff of the War Cabinet — his knowledge of occultism was reportedly consulted for deception operations
  - **Maxwell Knight** (MI5): ran agent networks that penetrated both far-left and occult groups in 1930s-40s Britain

### 2.3 The "Psychic Arms Race"
- During the Cold War, both superpowers feared the other was developing **psychic weapons**:
  - CIA documents cite Soviet parapsychology programs as justification for American research
  - Soviet researchers in turn cited American programs
  - This created a **self-reinforcing cycle** — each side's research justified the other's, regardless of whether psychic phenomena were real
  - The result was millions of dollars spent by both superpowers investigating phenomena that mainstream science does not accept

## 3. SPECULATIVE CLAIMS (Tier 3 — Possible but Unverified)

### 3.1 Ongoing Classified Programs
- The termination of Project Stargate in 1995 and MKUltra in 1973 does not necessarily mean that all intelligence interest in these areas ceased:
  - Some former participants (McMoneagle, Swann before his death) maintained that elements of the remote viewing program continued under different auspices
  - Without declassification, the existence of current programs cannot be confirmed or denied

## 4. DUBIOUS CLAIMS (Tier 4 — No Credible Source / Contradicted by Evidence)

### 4.1 Intelligence Agencies Successfully Created Mind-Controlled Assassins
- **[UNSUBSTANTIATED]** While MKUltra researched behavioral modification, no credible evidence indicates that the CIA (or any other agency) successfully created "Manchurian Candidate"-style programmed assassins. The 1977 Senate hearings revealed that MKUltra's results were largely inconclusive

### 4.2 Remote Viewing Produced Reliable Military Intelligence
- **[CONTESTED]** The 1995 AIR evaluation concluded that remote viewing could not be relied upon for intelligence purposes. While some individual sessions produced striking results, the overall program could not demonstrate consistent, actionable intelligence value
