## QUICK SUMMARY

**Inanna** (Sumerian: 𒀭𒈹, *d.INANNA*) / **Ishtar** (Akkadian: 𒀭𒌋𒁯, *d.IŠTAR*) is the most important goddess of ancient Mesopotamia — the divine personification of **love, sexuality, war, and political power**, identified with the planet **Venus** as both Morning and Evening Star. Attested continuously from the **Uruk period** (~3500 BCE) through the fall of Babylon (539 BCE) — spanning over 3,000 years — Inanna/Ishtar is documented in more surviving texts than any other Mesopotamian deity except Enlil and Marduk. Her key mythological narratives include: (1) **"Inanna and the ME"** — stealing the divine powers of civilization from Enki; (2) **"The Descent of Inanna"** — dying, entering the underworld, and returning; (3) **"Inanna and Dumuzi"** — the sacred marriage (*hieros gamos*) and its tragic aftermath; and (4) her role in the **Epic of Gilgamesh** (Tablet VI) where Gilgamesh rejects her sexual advances. Her cult center was the **Eanna** ("House of Heaven") temple at **Uruk**, one of the oldest monumental religious structures in human history. Inanna/Ishtar's theological influence extends through **Astarte** (Canaanite/Phoenician), **Aphrodite** (Greek), **Venus** (Roman), and arguably into Marian devotion (the "Queen of Heaven" title).

## §1 — PRIMARY TEXT EVIDENCE

### "Inanna and the ME" (Sumerian, ~2100–2000 BCE) [5/5 sources]

- Inanna travels to **Eridu** to visit **Enki** (god of wisdom). She gets Enki drunk at a feast and he gives her the **ME** — the divine decrees/programs that govern all aspects of civilization (→ [A06](../A_Foundations/A06_Sumerian_ME_Divine_Programs.md))
- She loads the ME onto her **Boat of Heaven** and takes them to Uruk. Enki sobers up and sends monsters to recover them, but Inanna's vizier **Ninshubur** successfully defends her
- The ME she takes include: *en*-ship (lordship), godship, the throne, the scepter, the art of lovemaking, the art of war, truth, descent to the underworld, ascent from the underworld, the craft of the smith, writing, and dozens more
- **Interpretation:** This myth explains how **Uruk** became the dominant Sumerian city — Inanna transferred the foundations of civilization from Eridu to Uruk. It also characterizes Inanna as a figure who acquires power through cunning, not inheritance
- **Tier 1** — ETCSL 1.3.1; multiple translations (Wolkstein & Kramer 1983; Black et al. 2004)

### "The Descent of Inanna" (Sumerian, ~1900–1750 BCE) [5/5 sources]

One of the most important myth cycles in world literature:

1. **Inanna decides** to descend to the **Kur** (underworld), ruled by her sister **Ereshkigal**. Her stated reason: to attend the funeral of Ereshkigal's husband Gugalanna (the Bull of Heaven). Her unstated reason: to extend her power over death itself
2. **Seven gates:** At each of the seven gates, Inanna is forced to remove one piece of regalia — crown, lapis necklace, breast stones, gold ring, measuring rod, breastplate, royal robe — entering naked and powerless before her sister
3. **Death:** Ereshkigal fixes Inanna with the "eye of death," and the Anunnaki judges condemn her. She is killed and hung as a corpse on a hook
4. **Rescue:** After three days and three nights, Inanna's faithful servant **Ninshubur** pleads with the gods. Enki creates two genderless beings (*kurgarra* and *galatur*) who bring Inanna back to life with the "food of life" and "water of life"
5. **The substitute:** Underworld law requires a substitute (*ki-sikil*). Inanna returns to earth accompanied by demons (*galla*) seeking her replacement. She finds her husband **Dumuzi** sitting on her throne celebrating instead of mourning — she condemns him to take her place
6. **Resolution:** Dumuzi's sister Geshtinanna volunteers to share his sentence — each spends half the year in the underworld (seasonal cycle etiology)

- **The stripping at seven gates** has been interpreted as: an initiation ritual, a metaphor for ego-death, a seasonal-vegetation cycle, and a power-structure analysis (you cannot take earthly authority into the realm of death)
- **Tier 1** — ETCSL 1.4.1; Dalley 1989; Wolkstein & Kramer 1983

### Akkadian "Descent of Ishtar" (~1st millennium BCE) [4/5 sources]

- Shorter version (137 lines vs. ~400+ in Sumerian); more focused on the cosmic consequences — when Ishtar is in the underworld, all sexual reproduction ceases on earth
- The Akkadian version emphasizes Ishtar's cosmic necessity: she is not just a deity but a **cosmic function** — without her, life itself stops
- **Tier 1** — Dalley 1989; Foster 2005

### Epic of Gilgamesh, Tablet VI [5/5 sources]

- After slaying Humbaba, Ishtar (Inanna) propositions **Gilgamesh** sexually
- Gilgamesh **rejects her**, listing her previous lovers and what she did to each: Dumuzi (sent to underworld), a bird (broken wing), a lion (dug pits), a horse (whip and spur), a shepherd (turned into a wolf), Ishullanu the gardener (turned into a frog/mole)
- Ishtar rages, goes to her father **Anu**, and demands the **Bull of Heaven** to destroy Gilgamesh; she threatens: "If you do not give me the Bull of Heaven, I will smash the doors of the underworld...and the dead will outnumber the living"
- **Tier 1** — George 2003 critical edition

## §2 — THE PLANET VENUS CONNECTION

### Astronomical Identification [5/5 sources]

- Inanna/Ishtar = the planet **Venus** — the third-brightest object in the sky after the Sun and Moon
- Venus's dual appearance as **Morning Star** (rising before dawn) and **Evening Star** (visible after sunset) was recognized by the 3rd millennium BCE and directly shaped Inanna's theology:
  - **Morning Star** = Inanna as **warrior goddess** (aggressive, war-bringing)
  - **Evening Star** = Inanna as **love/fertility goddess** (sexually receptive, abundance-bringing)
  - **Invisibility period** (when Venus is behind the Sun, ~8 days for superior conjunction or ~50 days for inferior conjunction) = Inanna **in the underworld**
- The **Venus Tablet of Ammi-ṣaduqa** (BM 86378, ~1650 BCE, Old Babylonian copy of older observations): The earliest surviving astronomical observation record — 21 years of Venus rising/setting data, explicitly connected to Ishtar omens. Listed as "If Ishtar on the 15th of Shabatu disappears in the west..." → omen text
- **Tier 1** — The astronomical identification is universal in Assyriology (Reiner & Pingree 1975)

## §3 — THE SACRED MARRIAGE (HIEROS GAMOS)

### The Ritual [4/5 sources]

- The **Sacred Marriage** was an annual ritual at Uruk (and possibly other cities) in which the king ritually copulated with a priestess representing Inanna, ensuring fertility, prosperity, and divine blessing for the city
- **Primary texts:** Sumerian love poetry attributed to Inanna and Dumuzi (Sefati 1998) — highly explicit erotic verse: "My vulva, the horn, the Boat of Heaven, is full of eagerness like the young moon — my untilled field lies fallow — who will plow my vulva? who will water my holy field?"
- **Archaeological context:** The Eanna temple complex at Uruk (→ earliest monumental architecture, ~3500 BCE) was the center of this cult; administrative tablets suggest temple personnel dedicated to Inanna's service, including the **nadītu** (cloistered women) and **kurgarra/galatur** (gender-variant ritual performers)
- **Debate:** Whether literal sexual intercourse occurred in the ritual, or whether it was purely symbolic/literary, remains unresolved. Lapinkivi (2004) and Cooper (2006) argue for varying degrees of literalness
- **Tier 2** — The texts exist; the degree of ritual enactment is debated

## §4 — GENDER AND BOUNDARY TRANSGRESSION

### Inanna as Boundary-Crosser [4/5 sources]

- Inanna/Ishtar systematically transgresses **every major binary category** in Mesopotamian thought:
  - **Gender:** Female deity who takes on male warrior roles; patron of gender-variant cultic personnel (*kurgarra, galatur, assinnu, kurgarrû*)
  - **Life/Death:** Descends to and returns from the underworld
  - **Love/War:** Goddess of both extremes simultaneously
  - **Morning/Evening:** Dual Venus identification
  - **Order/Chaos:** Acquires the ME (order) through trickster-like cunning (chaos)
- Her hymns explicitly celebrate this boundary-crossing: "To turn a man into a woman and a woman into a man are yours, Inanna" (Hymn to Inanna, lines 115-131)
- **Tier 2** — Scholarly analysis by Groneberg (1986), Harris (1991), and Peled (2016)

## §5 — TRANSMISSION AND COGNATE DEITIES

### From Inanna to Aphrodite [4/5 sources]

| Deity | Culture | Period | Key Attributes | Venus Connection |
|-------|---------|--------|----------------|-----------------|
| **Inanna** | Sumerian | ~3500–2000 BCE | Love, war, ME, underworld descent | Yes — explicit |
| **Ishtar** | Akkadian/Babylonian/Assyrian | ~2350–539 BCE | Love, war, queen of heaven, Bull of Heaven | Yes — explicit |
| **Astarte** / **Ashtoreth** | Canaanite/Phoenician | ~1500–300 BCE | Fertility, war, sacred sexuality | Yes — probable |
| **Aphrodite** | Greek | ~800 BCE onward | Love, beauty, sexuality | Yes — born from sea/sky |
| **Venus** | Roman | ~300 BCE onward | Love, beauty, political patronage | Yes — planet namesake |

- **Transmission pathway:** Inanna → Ishtar (Akkadian cultural absorption, not replacement) → Astarte (West Semitic, via Phoenician contact) → Aphrodite (Greek, via Cyprus — Aphrodite's birth at Paphos). Burkert (1985) and West (1997) document the cultural transmission chain
- **Biblical "Queen of Heaven":** Jeremiah 7:18, 44:17-25 condemns Judahites for offering cakes to the "Queen of Heaven" (*meleḵet ha-šāmayim*) — almost certainly a form of Ishtar/Astarte worship
- **Tier 2** — The transmission chain is mainstream scholarship; specific links are debated

## §6 — COUNTER-ARGUMENTS AND CRITICAL ASSESSMENT

### Against Over-Romanticization [3/5 sources]

- **Issue:** Popular treatments (especially Wolkstein & Kramer 1983, widely read) sometimes present Inanna as a straightforwardly empowering feminist figure. Actual Sumerian texts present a more complex deity: she is also terrifying, destructive, capricious, and associated with the suffering of her lovers
- **Counter:** The complexity is precisely the point — Inanna represents **uncontained divine power** that refuses to be tamed into any single category

### Against Ancient Astronaut Readings [2/5 sources]

- **Claim:** "Inanna's descent through seven gates represents passage through seven planetary spheres / dimensions" (Sitchin, von Däniken)
- **Assessment:** **Tier 4** — The seven-gate motif reflects Mesopotamian underworld geography (documented in other Sumerian texts with no astronomical context). The number seven had general sacred significance. No ancient text connects the descent gates to planets. This interpretation post-dates Babylonian astronomy by millennia

### Against Universal Goddess Theory [3/5 sources]

- **Claim:** All ancient goddesses are really "the Great Goddess" and Inanna is her primary form (Gimbutas 1989; Stone 1976)
- **Assessment:** **Tier 3** — This conflates vastly different deities with different attributes, cult practices, and cultural contexts. Inanna/Ishtar has specific characteristics (warrior aspect, Venus identification, descent narrative) that are NOT shared by all goddess figures. The homogenizing tendency distorts rather than illuminates. However, specific transmission chains (Inanna → Ishtar → Astarte → Aphrodite) are well-documented
