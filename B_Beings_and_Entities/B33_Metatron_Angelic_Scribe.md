## QUICK SUMMARY

**Metatron** is the highest-ranking angel in Jewish mystical tradition — variously called the **"Prince of the Countenance"** (*sar ha-panim*), the **"Lesser YHWH"** (*YHWH ha-qatan*), and the **heavenly scribe** who records the deeds of Israel. He appears most prominently in **3 Enoch** (*Sefer Hekhalot*, ~5th–6th century CE), where the patriarch **Enoch** (Genesis 5:24, "he walked with God; then he was no more") is transformed into Metatron upon his ascension to heaven. The **Babylonian Talmud** references Metatron in two crucial passages (Hagigah 15a and Sanhedrin 38b) that generated centuries of rabbinic debate about whether an exalted angel threatened strict monotheism. Metatron does not appear in the Hebrew Bible, the New Testament, or the Quran. His name is of **disputed etymology** — theories include Greek *metathronos* ("beside the throne"), Latin *metator* ("guide/measurer"), or a unique constructions from Hebrew/Aramaic. Metatron became central to **Kabbalistic** angel hierarchies (Zohar, Sefer ha-Bahir) and remains the most discussed angel in Jewish esoteric tradition, serving as the paradigmatic case of **human-to-angel transformation**.

## 1. PRIMARY TEXTUAL SOURCES

### 1.1 3 Enoch (Sefer Hekhalot)

- **3 Enoch** (Hebrew: ספר היכלות, *Sefer Hekhalot*, "Book of the Heavenly Palaces") — the primary text for Metatron
- Date: ~5th–6th century CE (scholarly consensus: Alexander, 1983; Schäfer, 2009); some material may be earlier
- **Narrator:** Rabbi Ishmael ascends through the seven heavens (Hekhalot) and encounters Metatron, who serves as his guide and reveals heavenly secrets
- **3 Enoch 3-15:** Metatron identifies himself as the transformed Enoch: "I was Enoch son of Jared... The Holy One, blessed be He, appointed me in the height as a prince and a ruler among the ministering angels"
- **3 Enoch 10:** Metatron's throne is placed at the door of the seventh Hekhal (palace); he is given a crown and 72 wings
- **3 Enoch 12:** Metatron is called **"Lesser YHWH"** — "because my name is like His name" — based on Exodus 23:21 ("my name is in him")
- **3 Enoch 48C:** Metatron is identified as the **heavenly scribe** who records the merits and demerits of Israel
- **Tier 1** — The text is critically edited and translated (Alexander in Charlesworth, OTP, vol. 1, 1983)

### 1.2 Babylonian Talmud

- **Hagigah 15a:** The heretic **Aher** (Elisha ben Abuya) ascends to heaven and sees Metatron sitting (angels normally stand in God's presence). Aher concludes: "Perhaps there are two powers in heaven!" (*shetei reshuyot*). God then punishes Metatron with 60 lashes of fire to demonstrate that Metatron is a servant, not a second deity
  - This passage crystallizes the theological danger: an exalted angel sitting near God's throne threatens the **"two powers in heaven" heresy** that rabbinic Judaism sought to suppress (→ Segal, 1977)
- **Sanhedrin 38b:** Metatron is identified as the angel in Exodus 23:20-21 ("I send an angel before you... attend to him... for my name is in him") — an angel bearing the divine Name
- **Tier 1** — Talmudic passages are canonical rabbinic texts, critically edited

### 1.3 2 Enoch (Slavonic Enoch)

- **2 Enoch** (~1st century CE, but surviving only in Slavonic MSS from 14th c. onward): Enoch ascends through seven heavens, meets angels, and is **transformed** — "the Lord said to Michael: 'Take Enoch and remove his earthly garments, anoint him with good oil, and dress him in the garments of glory'" (2 Enoch 22:8, longer recension)
- Although 2 Enoch does not use the name **"Metatron"**, it provides the theological foundation: Enoch's **bodily transformation into an angelic being** in God's presence
- **Tier 2** — 2 Enoch's date and original language are debated (Andersen, 1983; Böttrich, 1996)

## 2. IDENTITY AND TITLES

### 2.1 Metatron's Titles

| Title | Source | Meaning |
|-------|--------|---------|
| **Sar ha-Panim** (שר הפנים) | 3 Enoch, Hekhalot Rabbati | "Prince of the Countenance" — he sees God's face |
| **YHWH ha-Qatan** (יהוה הקטן) | 3 Enoch 12 | "Lesser YHWH" — his name bears the divine Name |
| **Na'ar** (נער) | Talmud, 3 Enoch | "Youth" — paradoxically applied despite his exalted rank |
| **Sofer** (סופר) | 3 Enoch 48C | "Scribe" — records Israel's deeds |
| **Mittator** / **Memator** | Targumic references | "Guide/mediator" — possibly related to Latin *metator* |
| **Prince of the World** | 3 Enoch 30 | Cosmic administrative role |
| **Prince of Torah** | Hekhalot Rabbati | Reveals Torah secrets to mystics |

### 2.2 Two Metatrons?

- **3 Enoch** presents **two traditions** about Metatron's origin:
  - **Metatron-Enoch:** Enoch transformed into Metatron (3 Enoch 3-15) — a **promoted human**
  - **Metatron as primordial angel:** Some passages suggest Metatron existed before creation — a **pre-existing celestial being**
- These may reflect the merging of two originally separate angelic traditions:
  - The **Enoch tradition** (→ [A04](../A_Foundations/A04_Book_of_Enoch_and_Watchers.md), [A25](../A_Foundations/A25_Slavonic_Enoch_2_Enoch_3_Enoch.md)) — human patriarch who ascended
  - A **separate exalted angel tradition** (perhaps related to the Angel of YHWH in Exodus, or to the "Son of Man" figure)
- Scholem (1960) argued these were originally separate figures merged in the Hekhalot literature
- **Tier 2** — Two-Metatron hypothesis is a scholarly reconstruction, debated

## 3. THE ENOCH-METATRON TRANSFORMATION

### 3.1 The Transformation Narrative (3 Enoch 6-15)

| Stage | Description | 3 Enoch Reference |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| 1 | Enoch ascends from earth on a **storm chariot** | 3 Enoch 6 |
| 2 | Ministering angels protest: "What is a human born of woman doing in the height?" | 3 Enoch 6:2 |
| 3 | God defends Enoch: "I have chosen him alone from among all of them" | 3 Enoch 6:3 |
| 4 | Enoch's body is **expanded**: "He enlarged my body to the size of the breadth and length of the world" | 3 Enoch 9 |
| 5 | **72 wings** and **365,000 eyes** are given to him | 3 Enoch 9:2-3 |
| 6 | His **flesh becomes flame**, his sinews fire, his bones coals, his eyes torches | 3 Enoch 15:1 |
| 7 | A **crown** is placed on his head with letters by which heaven and earth were created | 3 Enoch 13 |
| 8 | He is given the name "Lesser YHWH" | 3 Enoch 12:5 |
| 9 | He is seated on a **throne** at the door of the seventh palace | 3 Enoch 10:1 |

### 3.2 Theological Significance

- The Enoch-Metatron transformation establishes a **human-to-divine** ascent pattern rare in Judaism:
  - Genesis 5:24 ("Enoch walked with God; then he was no more, because God took him") is the seed text
  - The transformation goes far beyond what Genesis suggests — from "taken by God" to "made into the highest angel bearing God's own name"
- **Alan Segal** (*Two Powers in Heaven*, 1977) demonstrated that this angelic exaltation tradition was precisely what the rabbis of the Talmud were trying to control — the Hagigah 15a passage about Aher shows the danger: if an angel sits on a throne and bears YHWH's name, the door to binitarianism is open
- This has implications for **Christological origins** (→ scholars like Larry Hurtado, *Lord Jesus Christ*, 2003) — early Christian exaltation of Jesus may draw on the same "second power" traditions that produced Metatron
- **Tier 2** — Scholarly analysis with strong textual grounding; conclusions are debated

## 4. ETYMOLOGY AND NAME ORIGINS

### 4.1 Proposed Etymologies

| Etymology | Language | Meaning | Proposer(s) |
|-----------|----------|---------|-------------|
| **μετὰ θρόνον** (*meta thronon*) | Greek | "Beside the throne" or "after the throne" | Jellinek; Odeberg |
| **metator** | Latin | "Measurer, guide, pathfinder" (Roman military term for an advance scout) | Lieberman; Alexander |
| **מטטרון** as divine power name | Gnostic/magical | Constructed power name like other angel names (Sandalphon, etc.) | Scholem |
| **mat(t)ara** | Aramaic | "Guardian" or "keeper" | Margulies |
| **syncretic construction** | Multiple | An intentionally opaque name designed to sound powerful; no single etymology | Schäfer |

- **No etymology is universally accepted.** The Greek and Latin derivations are the most commonly cited in scholarship, but the name may have been constructed specifically to resist transparent etymology — mimicking the incomprehensible divine Name
- **Tier 2** — All etymologies are scholarly proposals; none is proven

## 5. METATRON IN KABBALISTIC AND LATER TRADITION

### 5.1 The Zohar and Kabbalah

- In the **Zohar** (~13th c., attributed to Shimon bar Yochai): Metatron is identified with the sefirah **Keter** (Crown) or with **Malkhut** (Kingdom) depending on the passage — he mediates between the upper and lower worlds
- Metatron serves as the **"angel of the Shekhinah"** — the manifestation of God's presence in the lower worlds
- The Zohar distinguishes between **"Metatron sar ha-panim"** (the higher Metatron) and a lower angelic Metatron who governs the mundane world
- In **Sefer ha-Bahir** (~12th c.): Metatron is associated with primordial light and the divine chariot
- **Tier 2** — Kabbalistic texts are documented; theological interpretations vary

### 5.2 Metatron's Cube

- **Metatron's Cube** — a geometric figure composed of 13 circles with lines connecting their centers, containing all five Platonic solids — is a **modern sacred geometry construction** attributed to Metatron
- This figure is NOT found in any ancient or medieval Jewish text — it appears in modern esoteric/New Age literature and sacred geometry writings
- The name "Metatron's Cube" appears to date from the **20th century** and has no basis in Kabbalistic sources
- **Tier 4** — Modern invention attributed anachronistically to ancient tradition

### 5.3 Sandalphon — Metatron's Twin

- In some traditions (Talmud Hagigah 13b), **Sandalphon** is Metatron's counterpart or "twin brother" — while Metatron was Enoch transformed, Sandalphon is sometimes identified with the prophet **Elijah** transformed
- Sandalphon gathers the prayers of Israel and weaves them into crowns for God
- The pairing creates a symmetry: one angel from the antediluvian era (Enoch/Metatron), one from the prophetic era (Elijah/Sandalphon)
- **Tier 2** — Textual basis exists but the pairing is not consistent across sources

## 6. CROSS-CULTURAL SCRIBE DEITY PARALLELS

### 6.1 Comparative Table

| Figure | Culture | Function | Human-to-Divine? | Record-Keeping? |
|--------|---------|----------|-------------------|-----------------|
| **Metatron** | Jewish mysticism | Heavenly scribe, angel of presence | Yes (Enoch transformed) | Yes — records Israel's deeds |
| **Thoth** | Egyptian | Scribe of the gods, recorder at judgment | No (always divine) | Yes — records at Judgment of Dead |
| **Nebo/Nabu** | Mesopotamian | Patron of scribes, recorder | No (always divine) | Yes — holds Tablets of Destiny |

- **Key distinction:** Metatron is the **only** major heavenly scribe figure who was **originally human** — this makes the Enoch-Metatron transformation theologically unique

## 7. COUNTER-ARGUMENTS AND CRITICAL ASSESSMENT

### 7.1 Against Early Dating of Metatron

- The name "Metatron" does not appear in **any** text before the Talmudic period (~3rd–5th c. CE) — he is absent from the Hebrew Bible, the Dead Sea Scrolls, 1 Enoch, Philo, Josephus, and the New Testament
- **3 Enoch** (the primary text) dates to ~5th–6th century CE — relatively late in the development of Jewish apocalyptic and mystical literature
- The Enoch-Metatron identification may be a **late synthesis** combining older Enoch traditions with a separate exalted angel figure
- **Tier 1** — Chronological evidence from textual attestation

### 7.2 Against Two-Powers Interpretation

- Mainstream rabbinic Judaism consistently treated Metatron as a **servant**, not a deity — the Hagigah 15a passage exists precisely to deny that Metatron is a second god
- The "Lesser YHWH" title may be hyperbolic honorific rather than ontological claim — similar to earthly kings being called "image of God" without implying divinity
- Modern scholars debate whether the Metatron traditions actually influenced early Christology or reflect parallel but independent developments
- **Tier 2** — Genuine scholarly disagreement

### 7.3 Popular Misrepresentations

- Metatron is frequently cited in New Age literature as the "highest angel in all traditions" — but he is **exclusively Jewish** in origin and is unknown in Christian angelology before modern syncretism
- "Metatron's Cube" has no medieval or ancient source
- Claims that Metatron appears in the Bible or Quran are incorrect — he is a post-biblical figure
- **Tier 1** — Correcting factual errors in popular literature
