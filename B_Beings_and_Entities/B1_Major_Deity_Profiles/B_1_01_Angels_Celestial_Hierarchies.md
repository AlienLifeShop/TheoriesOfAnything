## QUICK SUMMARY

**Angels** (from Greek *angelos* = "messenger," translating Hebrew *mal'akh*) appear in virtually every religious tradition — intermediary beings between the divine and human realms who carry messages, enforce divine will, guard sacred spaces, and execute cosmic functions. This document surveys the **major angelological traditions** across cultures: the Hebrew Bible's evolving angel concept (from anonymous messengers to named archangels → [A_2_03](../../A_Foundations/A2_Biblical_Gnostic_Western_Esoteric/A_2_03_Book_of_Enoch_and_Watchers.md)); **Pseudo-Dionysius's** influential 5th-century systematization into **nine orders** (seraphim, cherubim, thrones, dominions, virtues, powers, principalities, archangels, angels); **Islamic malā'ika** created from light, with strictly no free will (→ [A_4_06](../../A_Foundations/A4_Asian_Indigenous_Eastern/A_4_06_Quranic_Cosmology_Jinn_Angelology.md)); **Zoroastrian Amesha Spentas** — not "angels" exactly, but divine emanations of Ahura Mazda (→ [C_5_04](../../C_Global_Traditions/C5_Regional_Analytical_Traditions/C_5_04_Zoroastrianism_Demonization_Pivot.md)); and parallels in **Hindu devas**, **Buddhist devas**, **Tibetan dharmapalas**, and **Japanese tennin**. The angelic concept is complementary to the divine council (→ [A_1_05](../../A_Foundations/A1_Mesopotamian_Near_Eastern/A_1_05_Divine_Council_Assembly_of_Gods.md)) and the Watchers tradition (→ [A_2_03](../../A_Foundations/A2_Biblical_Gnostic_Western_Esoteric/A_2_03_Book_of_Enoch_and_Watchers.md)), and intersects with modern UAP experiencer reports of luminous beings (→ [B_2_05](../B2_Humanoid_Crypto_Entities/B_2_05_Alien_Races_Origins.md)).

## 1. ANGELS IN THE HEBREW BIBLE AND JUDAISM

### 1.1 Biblical Angel Evolution

| Period | Angel Concept | Key Texts |
|--------|--------------|-----------|
| **Early patriarchal** | Anonymous messengers indistinguishable from God himself; "angel of YHWH" may be a theophany | Genesis 18 (three visitors); Exodus 3 (burning bush); Judges 13 (Manoah's visitor) |
| **Monarchic** | Angels as divine court attendants; seraphim and cherubim as throne guardians | Isaiah 6 (seraphim); 1 Kings 22 (divine council); Ezekiel 1, 10 (cherubim/living creatures) |
| **Post-exilic** | Named individual angels emerge; Persian influence suspected | Daniel 8:16, 9:21 (Gabriel); Daniel 10:13, 12:1 (Michael); Zechariah 1-6 (interpreting angel) |
| **Second Temple** | Elaborate angelology; angel ranks, names, functions proliferate | 1 Enoch 6-36 (Watchers, 200 fallen angels → [A_2_03](../../A_Foundations/A2_Biblical_Gnostic_Western_Esoteric/A_2_03_Book_of_Enoch_and_Watchers.md)); Jubilees; Dead Sea Scrolls (→ [A_2_04](../../A_Foundations/A2_Biblical_Gnostic_Western_Esoteric/A_2_04_Dead_Sea_Scrolls_Expanded.md)); Tobit (Raphael) |
| **Rabbinic** | Angels formalized; Metatron as highest angel; seven archangels standard | Talmud, Hekhalot literature, 3 Enoch |

### 1.2 The Cherubim and Seraphim

| Being | Hebrew/Etymology | Appearance | Function |
|-------|-----------------|------------|----------|
| **Cherubim** (כְּרוּבִים) | Possibly from Akkadian *karābu* (to bless/pray) or *kāribu* (guardian spirit) | Multi-faced (human, lion, ox, eagle); winged; NOT the chubby babies of later art | Guard Eden (Gen 3:24); throne-bearers of God (Ezekiel 1, 10); Ark of the Covenant lid |
| **Seraphim** (שְׂרָפִים) | Hebrew *śārap* = "to burn" → "burning ones" | Six wings: two covering face, two covering feet, two for flying (Isaiah 6:2) | Attend God's throne; cry "Holy, holy, holy"; one purifies Isaiah's lips with a coal |
| **Ophanim** (אוֹפַנִּים) | Hebrew *'ophan* = "wheel" | Wheels within wheels, covered with eyes (Ezekiel 1:15-21) | Part of the divine throne mechanism; later ranked by Maimonides as second-highest order |

### 1.3 Merkabah Angels

The **Merkabah** (Chariot) tradition developed from Ezekiel's visions:
- **Hayyot** (living creatures) — four-faced beings carrying the throne
- **Ophanim** — the terrifying wheels
- **Metatron** — "Prince of the Presence" or "Lesser YHWH" — possibly transformed Enoch (→ [A_2_03](../../A_Foundations/A2_Biblical_Gnostic_Western_Esoteric/A_2_03_Book_of_Enoch_and_Watchers.md))
- Hekhalot literature describes **seven heavenly palaces** guarded by fearsome angels
- The mystic must know **names and seals** to pass each guardian — esoteric password system

## 2. CHRISTIAN ANGELOLOGY

### 2.1 Pseudo-Dionysius's Nine Orders

The most influential angelic hierarchy, from *De Coelesti Hierarchia* (~5th century CE):

| Sphere | Order | Function | Biblical Basis |
|--------|-------|----------|---------------|
| **First** (closest to God) | 1. **Seraphim** | Burning love; continuous praise; "Holy, holy, holy" | Isaiah 6 |
| | 2. **Cherubim** | Perfect knowledge; wisdom; throne-guardians | Ezekiel 1, 10; Genesis 3:24 |
| | 3. **Thrones** (Ophanim) | Divine justice; seats of God's authority | Colossians 1:16; Ezekiel 1 |
| **Second** (cosmic governors) | 4. **Dominions** (Kyriotetes) | Regulate duties of lower angels; channel divine energy | Colossians 1:16; Ephesians 1:21 |
| | 5. **Virtues** (Dynameis) | Bestow blessings; perform miracles; govern nature | 1 Peter 3:22; Ephesians 1:21 |
| | 6. **Powers** (Exousiai) | Maintain cosmic order; fight demonic forces | Colossians 1:16; Ephesians 6:12 |
| **Third** (messengers to humanity) | 7. **Principalities** (Archai) | Guard nations, cities, and rulers; oversee earthly regions | Colossians 1:16; Ephesians 3:10 |
| | 8. **Archangels** | Lead angel armies; deliver major divine messages | Jude 1:9; 1 Thessalonians 4:16 |
| | 9. **Angels** | Personal guardians; deliver routine messages to individuals | Throughout Bible; Matthew 18:10 |

### 2.2 Named Archangels

| Name | Tradition | Role |
|------|-----------|------|
| **Michael** | Judaism, Christianity, Islam | Commander of heavenly armies; dragon-slayer (Revelation 12); patron of Israel |
| **Gabriel** | Judaism, Christianity, Islam | Messenger of God; announces to Daniel, Zechariah, Mary; brings Quran to Muhammad |
| **Raphael** | Judaism, Christianity | Healer; guide in the Book of Tobit; one of seven who stand before God |
| **Uriel** | Judaism, some Christianity | "Light of God"; guards Eden; guides Enoch; appears in 4 Ezra |
| **Metatron** | Judaism (mystical) | "Prince of the Presence"; scribe of heaven; possibly transformed Enoch |
| **Azrael** | Islam (primarily) | Angel of Death (Malak al-Mawt) |
| **Israfil** | Islam | Trumpet-blower at Judgment Day |
| **Samael** | Judaism (complex) | "Poison of God"; angel of death; sometimes identified with Satan |

## 4. ZOROASTRIAN CELESTIAL BEINGS

### 4.1 Amesha Spentas — "Holy Immortals"

| Amesha Spenta | Domain | Parallel |
|---------------|--------|----------|
| **Vohu Manah** | Good Mind / Thought | Comparable to Wisdom/Sophia |
| **Asha Vahishta** | Best Righteousness / Truth | Cosmic order principle |
| **Khshathra Vairya** | Desirable Dominion | Sovereignty and metals |
| **Spenta Armaiti** | Holy Devotion / Earth | Earth and piety |
| **Haurvatat** | Wholeness / Health | Water and perfection |
| **Ameretat** | Immortality | Plants and eternal life |

### 4.2 Yazatas — "Beings Worthy of Worship"

- **Mithra** — judge, protector of covenants, light-bringer (→ major Roman cult)
- **Anahita** — divine feminine, waters, fertility
- **Sraosha** — obedience, guardian of prayer
- **Rashnu** — cosmic judge at the Chinvat Bridge
- These beings are NOT "gods" in Zoroastrian theology — they are emanations of Ahura Mazda
