## QUICK SUMMARY

**Primordial water entities** — personified cosmic oceans, abyssal waters, and aquatic chaos-beings from which the ordered universe emerges — represent one of the most universal cosmogonic motifs. In Mesopotamia, the **Apsu** (Abzu, the primordial freshwater abyss) and **Tiamat** (the primordial salt-water sea) are the original divine pair from whose mingling all other gods are born — Tiamat is subsequently slain by Marduk and her body used to construct the cosmos (the *Enuma Elish*). In Egypt, **Nun** (Nw, the primordial waters) is the undifferentiated, infinite, dark ocean preceding and surrounding creation — every Egyptian cosmogony begins with the emergence of a creator god (Atum at Heliopolis, Ptah at Memphis) from Nun. The Vedic **Varuna** originally governs the cosmic waters of the sky and the deep, associated with *ṛta* (cosmic order), while the Rigvedic creation hymn (10.129) describes existence before creation as water: "In the beginning there was darkness hidden by darkness… all this was water" (nāsadīya sūkta). The Hebrew **Tehom** (תְּהוֹם, "the deep," Genesis 1:2) — linguistically related to Tiamat — represents the primordial waters over which God's spirit hovers before creation. The Greek **Oceanus** (Ωκεανός) — the world-encircling river/ocean, "origin of gods and all things" (Homer, *Iliad* 14.246) — represents the Greek version. This document profiles the major primordial water entities and analyzes the cosmogonic water-to-order pattern.

## 1. VERIFIED CLAIMS (Tier 1 — Peer-Reviewed / Archaeological Record)

### 1.1 Mesopotamian Apsu and Tiamat
- **Apsu** (*Abzu* in Sumerian): The primordial freshwater abyss — the underground ocean from which springs, rivers, and life-giving water emerge
  - In Sumerian theology: the **Abzu** at **Eridu** (the oldest Sumerian city) was the sacred domain of **Enki** (Ea) — the temple of Enki was called the **E-abzu** ("House of the Abzu")
  - In the *Enuma Elish* (c. 12th century BCE): Apsu is personified as a male deity — the primordial father who mingles with Tiamat to produce the younger gods; when the noise of the younger gods disturbs him, Apsu plots their destruction; **Ea (Enki) kills Apsu** and builds his dwelling upon the slain god's waters
- **Tiamat** (𒀭𒋾𒊩𒆳): Personified primordial **salt-water ocean** — the cosmic mother
  - Tiamat creates an army of monsters (including the first dragons, scorpion-men, and storm beasts) to avenge Apsu
  - **Marduk** defeats Tiamat by driving a storm-wind into her open mouth, piercing her belly with an arrow, and splitting her body: **one half becomes the sky, the other the earth** — the cosmos as structured chaos, order imposed on the dragon's carcass
  - The *Enuma Elish* was recited at the **Akitu** (New Year) festival in Babylon — the ritual re-enactment of creation from chaos-water

### 1.2 Egyptian Nun
- **Nun** (Nw, njwn): The primordial watery abyss — personified as a deity but more fundamentally a **state of being**: undifferentiated, dark, infinite, motionless water existing before creation and coexisting with the created world (the waters "at the edges" of the cosmos)
  - Every major Egyptian cosmogony begins with emergence from Nun:
    - **Heliopolis**: Atum emerges from Nun on the **Benben** (primordial mound) and begins creation
    - **Hermopolis**: The **Ogdoad** (eight primordial deities including Nun and Naunet, his female counterpart) collectively generate creation from the waters
    - **Memphis**: Ptah exists within Nun and creates through thought and speech
  - Nun is depicted as a **bearded man standing waist-deep in water**, raising the solar barque — the moment of creation is the moment the sun first rises from the waters
  - Critically, Nun is **never destroyed** — the primordial waters persist, surrounding and underlying the created world; Egyptian cosmology maintains the possibility that creation could return to Nun — a latent eschatological anxiety

### 1.3 Hebrew Tehom
- **Tehom** (תְּהוֹם): "The deep" — Genesis 1:2: "The earth was without form and void (*tohu va-bohu*), and darkness was upon the face of the deep (*tehom*), and the spirit of God moved upon the face of the waters"
  - The **linguistic relationship** between *tehom* and *Tiamat* is well-established — both derive from the Semitic root *thm* (deep/sea) — though *tehom* in Genesis is **not personified or combated** (unlike Tiamat, God simply speaks over the waters) — representing either a de-mythologized version of the combat myth or an independent development from common vocabulary (Tsumura 2005)
  - The primordial deep does appear in **combat form** elsewhere in the Hebrew Bible: **Rahab** (Job 26:12, Isaiah 51:9), **Leviathan** (Psalm 74:14, Isaiah 27:1), and **Yam** ("Sea," Psalm 89:10) — divine monsters associated with primordial chaos waters that God defeats or controls
  - These passages suggest that Israelite theology **preserved and adapted** the Mesopotamian chaoskampf (chaos-combat) tradition while the Genesis account represents a deliberate theological revision — creation by word rather than by combat

### 1.4 Vedic Cosmogonic Waters
- **Rigveda 10.129** (*Nāsadīya Sūkta*, "Hymn of Creation"): "In the beginning, there was neither being nor non-being… all was water, undifferentiated" — one of the most philosophically sophisticated creation texts in world literature
  - The waters (āpas) are the primordial substance from which the universe emerges through desire (*kāma*) — "desire, the first seed of mind, arose"
- **Varuna** in the Rigveda: Guardian of the cosmic waters (*ṛta*, cosmic order) — associated with the night sky AND the celestial/subterranean ocean; his "nooses" bind transgressors
  - Varuna's later demotion to merely "lord of the ocean" (post-Vedic period) represents a theological narrowing — from cosmic sovereign over all waters to ruler of physical seas

## 2. CREDIBLE CLAIMS (Tier 2 — Academic / Debated but Supported)

### 2.1 Greek Oceanus and Tethys
- **Oceanus** (Ωκεανός): The world-encircling river — Homer calls him "the origin of the gods and origin of all things" (*Iliad* 14.246) — making Homer's Oceanus a **cosmogonic primordial water** comparable to Nun and Apsu
  - Paired with **Tethys** (Τηθύς) as the primordial water couple — paralleling Apsu-Tiamat (pre-cosmic water in gendered pairs)
  - Hesiod's *Theogony* lists Oceanus as a Titan — the transition from cosmogonic principle to genealogical figure reflects Greek theology's rationalization of older mythic material

### 2.2 Sumerian Nammu
- **Nammu** (𒀭𒇉): Sumerian primordial mother goddess — "the mother who gave birth to heaven and earth" and "the mother who gave birth to all the gods" — associated with primordial waters
  - Nammu may be the **earliest named primordial water entity** in the Sumerian textual record (earlier than Tiamat, who is Babylonian/Akkadian)
  - Her relationship to later Tiamat is debated — they may represent successive theological developments of the same primordial-water-as-mother concept

### 2.3 African and Polynesian Primordial Oceans
- **Olokun** (Yoruba): Deity of the deep ocean — powerful, dangerous, and associated with wealth from the sea floor; venerated in Benin Kingdom art (bronze plaques depicting Olokun with mudfish legs)
- **Polynesian void-ocean**: In multiple Polynesian cosmogonies, the universe begins with **Te Kore** (the Void) or **Te Po** (the Darkness), which is also imagined as an expanse of water — from which the world is fished up (Maui fishing islands from the sea) or separated (Rangi and Papa)

## 3. SPECULATIVE CLAIMS (Tier 3 — Possible but Unverified)

### 3.1 Geological Memory Hypothesis
- The universality of primordial water cosmogonies has prompted the question: do they encode a genuine **collective memory** of post-glacial flooding (sea-level rise of ~120 meters since the Last Glacial Maximum, c. 20,000 BP)? The submergence of vast coastal areas could theoretically have entered deep mythology
- While the geological events are real, the mythological connection is impossible to demonstrate — cosmogonic water imagery more likely reflects the observable reality that **water is the universal medium of life** and the most accessible symbol of formlessness

### 3.2 Chaos-Water as Pre-Conceptual State
- Cognitive science interpretations (Slingerland 2008) suggest that "chaos water" represents the mind's natural model of a **pre-conceptual, undifferentiated state** — the formless condition before cognitive categories are applied — making primordial water cosmogonies a metaphor for the process of thought itself
