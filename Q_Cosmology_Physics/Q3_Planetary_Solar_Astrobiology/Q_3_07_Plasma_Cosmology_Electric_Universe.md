## QUICK SUMMARY

**Plasma cosmology** and its populist extension, the **Electric Universe (EU)** hypothesis, propose that **electromagnetic forces** — rather than gravity — are the dominant organizing force in the cosmos, and that **plasma** (ionized gas, constituting ~99% of visible matter in the universe) shapes cosmic structures through electrical and magnetic interactions. The scientific roots lie in the work of **Kristian Birkeland** (1867-1917), who demonstrated that auroras are caused by **electrical currents** from the Sun channeled along Earth's magnetic field lines ("Birkeland currents"), and **Hannes Alfvén** (1908-1995, **Nobel Prize 1970** for magnetohydrodynamics), who emphasized the importance of electromagnetic forces in astrophysical plasmas and criticized the Big Bang model's handling of plasma physics. Alfvén's concerns were legitimate and contributed to better plasma modeling in standard cosmology. However, the **Electric Universe** movement (Thornhill, Talbott, and online communities) extends these ideas far beyond what the evidence supports, claiming that: **gravity is a secondary force**; the Sun is electrically powered (not by fusion); **comets are electrically discharged**; **planets have recently changed orbits** (Velikovskian influence); **the Big Bang never happened**; **dark matter/dark energy don't exist** (electric forces explain galaxy rotation); and **ancient petroglyphs depict plasma discharge events** in Earth's sky (Peratt's hypothesis). While plasma physics is rigorous science (Tier 1), and Alfvén raised valid criticisms (Tier 2), the Electric Universe as a comprehensive alternative cosmology is **not supported by the weight of evidence** (Tier 3) — it fails to account for the CMB, Big Bang nucleosynthesis, galaxy cluster dynamics, and gravitational wave observations.

## 1. PLASMA PHYSICS — THE SOLID FOUNDATION

### 1.1 What Is Plasma?

Plasma — the fourth state of matter — is ionized gas containing free electrons and ions. It exhibits **collective behavior** mediated by long-range electromagnetic forces:
- **~99.9% of visible matter** in the universe is plasma (stars, nebulae, interstellar/intergalactic medium)
- Plasmas are **electrically conductive**, carry currents, generate magnetic fields, and exhibit **filamentary structure** (self-organized current channels)
- Plasma behavior is governed by **magnetohydrodynamics (MHD)** and kinetic plasma theory
- Plasmas are **scalable** — similar structures appear from laboratory to cosmic scales

### 1.2 Key Plasma Phenomena

| Phenomenon | Description | Cosmic Example |
|------------|-------------|----------------|
| **Birkeland currents** | Field-aligned electrical currents in magnetized plasma | Auroras; solar wind-magnetosphere coupling; galactic filaments |
| **Z-pinch** | Axial current compresses plasma radially via self-generated magnetic field | Star formation in filaments; laboratory fusion devices |
| **Double layers** | Thin regions of charge separation in plasma; accelerate particles; emit radiation | Solar flares; auroral acceleration regions |
| **Plasma instabilities** | Rayleigh-Taylor, Kelvin-Helmholtz, kink/sausage instabilities | Solar prominences; jet structures; nebular morphology |
| **Magnetic reconnection** | Magnetic field lines break and reconnect, releasing energy explosively | Solar flares; magnetospheric substorms; astrophysical jets |

## 2. ALFVÉN'S PLASMA COSMOLOGY — LEGITIMATE SCIENCE

### 2.1 Hannes Alfvén's Contributions

Alfvén won the **1970 Nobel Prize in Physics** for discoveries in magnetohydrodynamics:
- **Alfvén waves** — magnetic waves propagating through conducting fluids/plasmas
- Demonstrated the importance of **electromagnetic forces** in astrophysics (previously underestimated)
- Emphasized that laboratory plasma experiments should inform cosmic plasma models

### 2.2 Alfvén's Cosmological Critiques

Alfvén raised legitimate concerns about standard cosmology:
- The Big Bang model (as formulated in the 1960s-80s) underestimated plasma effects in the early universe
- **Cosmic filamentary structure** (galaxies arranged in filaments/walls/voids) resembles plasma filament structures
- Standard models relied too heavily on gravity alone for structure formation
- Alfvén proposed a **matter-antimatter cosmology** as an alternative to the Big Bang

**Status**: Many of Alfvén's plasma physics insights were **incorporated into** standard astrophysics. His cosmological alternatives were **not confirmed**: the CMB, Big Bang nucleosynthesis (BBN), and cosmic expansion observations strongly favor the Big Bang/ΛCDM model.

### 3.2 Why EU Persists Despite Evidence

The Electric Universe movement persists due to:
- **Genuine complexity** of standard cosmology (dark matter, dark energy remain mysterious → legitimate frustration)
- **Real observational surprises** (cosmic filaments; unexpected comet activity; EU interprets these as supporting evidence)
- **Internet community** dynamics; contrarian identity; critique of "establishment science"
- **Conflation** of legitimate plasma astrophysics with unsupported EU claims
- **Aesthetic appeal** — the universe as a coherent electrical system feels more intuitive than abstract dark matter/dark energy

## 4. WHAT STANDARD COSMOLOGY ACTUALLY DOES WITH PLASMA

Modern astrophysics fully incorporates plasma physics:
- **Magnetohydrodynamic simulations** of galaxy formation, stellar evolution, accretion disks, jets
- **Solar physics** is fundamentally plasma physics (MHD models of corona, flares, solar wind)
- **Cosmic magnetic fields** are measured and modeled (Faraday rotation, synchrotron emission, CMB polarization)
- **Intergalactic medium** is modeled as magnetized plasma; Birkeland currents are standard astrophysics vocabulary
- The legitimate insights of Alfvén and Birkeland have been **absorbed** into mainstream astrophysics

## Counter-Arguments & Criticisms

The Electric Universe hypothesis and plasma cosmology have been extensively criticized by mainstream astrophysics. These models cannot account for the cosmic microwave background radiation’s blackbody spectrum, precisely predicted by standard Big Bang cosmology (Peebles, 1993). Plasma cosmology fails to explain primordial nucleosynthesis—the observed ratios of hydrogen, helium, and lithium that match Lambda-CDM predictions (Fields & Olive, 2006). The claim that electromagnetic forces dominate over gravity at cosmic scales contradicts gravitational lensing observations, galaxy rotation curve data, and the concordance of multiple independent cosmological measurements. Physicist Tim Thompson has published detailed critiques noting that Electric Universe models violate established electromagnetic theory and thermodynamic principles. The mainstream astrophysics community notes that plasma cosmology proponents have not published their models in peer-reviewed astrophysical journals or subjected them to standard observational tests required of competing cosmological theories.
