## QUICK SUMMARY

The Nazi regime conducted **two parallel campaigns of cultural destruction and theft** between 1933 and 1945: the **public burning and censorship of books** deemed "un-German" (*undeutsch*) beginning with the **May 10, 1933 book burnings** across German university cities, and the **systematic looting of art and cultural property** from conquered territories and Jewish populations — the largest organized art theft in history. The book burnings, orchestrated by the **German Student Union** (*Deutsche Studentenschaft*) with regime support, destroyed an estimated **25,000 volumes** on the first night alone, targeting works by Jewish, socialist, pacifist, and sexually liberal authors. The art looting, primarily conducted through the **Einsatzstab Reichsleiter Rosenberg (ERR)** and **Hermann Göring's personal agents**, confiscated an estimated **600,000 artworks** from museums, galleries, and private collections across occupied Europe. The Allied **Monuments, Fine Arts, and Archives (MFAA) program** — the "Monuments Men" — recovered vast quantities, but an estimated **100,000 artworks remain missing** as of 2025, and restitution cases continue today.

## §1 — THE BOOK BURNINGS OF MAY 1933

### The "Action Against the Un-German Spirit"

- The book burnings were organized by the **German Student Union** (*Deutsche Studentenschaft*) as part of a coordinated campaign called **"Aktion wider den undeutschen Geist"** ("Action Against the Un-German Spirit")
- The campaign began April 12, 1933 with the publication of **"Twelve Theses Against the Un-German Spirit"** — posted on university notice boards, explicitly modeled on Luther's Ninety-Five Theses
- **May 10, 1933:** Simultaneous book burnings were held in **34 university cities** across Germany — the largest at the **Opernplatz** (now Bebelplatz) in Berlin, where approximately 20,000 books were burned before a crowd of 40,000
- **Joseph Goebbels**, Reich Minister of Propaganda, gave a speech at the Berlin burning: "The era of extreme Jewish intellectualism is now at an end... The future German man will not just be a man of books, but a man of character"
- The burnings were **public spectacles** — torchlight processions, ceremonial fire oaths ("Feuersprüche"), and coordinated media coverage; the performative dimension was as important as the actual destruction

### Targeted Authors and Categories

- The student union issued a **blacklist** organized by category. Targeted works included:

| Category | Representative Authors |
|----------|----------------------|
| **Marxism and socialism** | Karl Marx, Karl Kautsky, Rosa Luxemburg |
| **Psychoanalysis** | Sigmund Freud (who reportedly commented: "What progress we are making. In the Middle Ages they would have burnt me; nowadays they are content with burning my books") |
| **Sexology** | Magnus Hirschfeld (see §2) |
| **Pacifism** | Erich Maria Remarque (*All Quiet on the Western Front*), Kurt Tucholsky |
| **Jewish literature** | Franz Kafka, Stefan Zweig, Lion Feuchtwanger |
| **Liberal/democratic theory** | Thomas Mann, Heinrich Mann, Hugo Preuss (drafter of the Weimar Constitution) |
| **American authors** | Ernest Hemingway, Jack London, Helen Keller, H.G. Wells |

- Helen Keller wrote an open letter to the German Student Union: "History has taught you nothing if you think you can kill ideas. Tyrants have tried to do that often before, and the ideas have risen and destroyed them"
- **Tier 1** — The burnings were photographed, filmed, and reported internationally in real time

### The Institut für Sexualwissenschaft

- **May 6, 1933** (four days before the main burnings): SA troops and student groups raided the **Institut für Sexualwissenschaft** (Institute for Sexual Science), founded by **Dr. Magnus Hirschfeld** in 1919 — the world's first institution dedicated to the study of human sexuality
- The institute's library — containing **over 20,000 books** and **35,000 photographs** — was confiscated; approximately **10,000 volumes** were burned publicly on May 10
- The institute held pioneering research on homosexuality, transgender identity, intersex conditions, contraception, and sexually transmitted diseases — much of this research was **permanently lost**
- Hirschfeld was out of Germany at the time and never returned; he died in exile in Nice in 1935
- The destruction of the institute is now recognized as one of the **earliest targeted attacks** in the Nazi persecution of sexual and gender minorities (later formalized through expanded **Paragraph 175** enforcement)
- **Tier 1** — Photographic evidence, survivor accounts, institutional records

## §2 — "DEGENERATE ART" AND CULTURAL PURGING

### The Entartete Kunst Campaign

- The Nazi regime categorized modern art as **"degenerate" (entartet)** — a term borrowed from Max Nordau's *Entartung* (1892) and weaponized against artistic movements that challenged classical, representational, and "Germanic" aesthetics
- **July 19, 1937:** The **"Entartete Kunst"** exhibition opened in Munich — displaying confiscated works by **Ernst Ludwig Kirchner, Paul Klee, Wassily Kandinsky, Otto Dix, Max Beckmann, Marc Chagall, Piet Mondrian**, and others, deliberately hung crookedly and accompanied by mocking labels
- The exhibition was designed to ridicule modern art and drew **over 2 million visitors** — paradoxically, it was the most-attended art exhibition in history at the time
- Simultaneously, the **"Große Deutsche Kunstausstellung"** (Great German Art Exhibition) displayed approved art — classical, heroic, landscape, and racial-ideological works
- An estimated **16,000 works** were confiscated from German public museums and collections — some were sold abroad for foreign currency, some destroyed

### The 1939 Destruction

- **March 20, 1939:** Approximately **1,004 paintings and sculptures** and **3,825 works on paper** (watercolors, prints, drawings) were reportedly burned at the **Berlin Fire Department headquarters** — though the exact count and whether all were actually destroyed remains debated
- Some works were secretly retained by Nazi officials, sold through dealers like **Karl Buchholz** and **Hildebrand Gurlitt** (whose son **Cornelius Gurlitt** was discovered in 2012 with ~1,500 works, many from the confiscation program — the "Gurlitt trove")
- Works by Kirchner, Nolde, Schmidt-Rottluff, and many others were permanently lost
- **Tier 1** — Commission records, sales documentation, and the Gurlitt discovery

## §3 — SYSTEMATIC ART LOOTING IN OCCUPIED EUROPE

### The ERR (Einsatzstab Reichsleiter Rosenberg)

- The **ERR** was established in 1940 by **Alfred Rosenberg** (Nazi ideologue, later convicted at Nuremberg) as an organized looting operation with pseudo-academic justification
- The ERR systematically confiscated artworks, libraries, archives, and cultural objects from:
  - **Jewish collections:** Private collections of prominent Jewish families (Rothschild, David-Weill, Bernheim-Jeune, Schloss, Kann, Seligmann, and many others)
  - **Museums and galleries** in occupied France, Belgium, Netherlands, Poland, and the Soviet Union
  - **Masonic lodges, churches, universities**, and cultural institutions
- The ERR processed confiscated art through the **Jeu de Paume museum** in Paris — art historian **Rose Valland** secretly documented the ERR's activities, recording which works were taken where, which became critical for post-war recovery
- The ERR compiled detailed **inventories and photographs** — this meticulous documentation, intended for bureaucratic control, became the primary evidence for post-war restitution

### Göring's Personal Looting

- **Hermann Göring** used his position as head of the Luftwaffe and second-ranking Nazi to conduct **personal art acquisition** on a massive scale
- Göring had "first pick" of ERR confiscations — choosing works for his personal collection at **Carinhall**, his hunting lodge north of Berlin
- He acquired an estimated **1,500+ works of art** through confiscation, coerced sales, and exchanges (trading "degenerate" modern art for Old Masters)
- His collection included works by Vermeer, Rembrandt, Cranach, Rubens, and many others — the Vermeer turned out to include the famous **Han van Meegeren forgery** (*Christ with the Woman Taken in Adultery*)
- **Tier 1** — Göring's activities are documented in Nuremberg trial records and ERR inventories

### Scale of the Theft

- **France alone:** The ERR and related operations confiscated from **approximately 200 Jewish-owned collections** — the Rosenberg Special Staff processed an estimated **22,000 objects** through the Jeu de Paume
- **Total across occupied Europe:** Estimates range from **600,000 to over 750,000 cultural objects** (paintings, sculptures, furniture, manuscripts, rare books, musical instruments, Judaica)
- **The Führermuseum project:** Hitler planned a massive art museum in **Linz, Austria** (his hometown) — the "Linz Collection" or "Sonderauftrag Linz" was to be the world's greatest art museum, stocked with looted masterpieces
- **Mine storage:** Looted art was stored in salt mines (notably **Altaussee**, Austria), castles, and bunkers — the Altaussee mine contained an estimated **6,500 paintings** including Jan van Eyck's *Ghent Altarpiece* and Vermeer's *The Astronomer*
- **Tier 1** — ERR inventories, Nuremberg evidence, mine recovery records

## §4 — THE MONUMENTS MEN AND RECOVERY

### MFAA Operations

- The **Monuments, Fine Arts, and Archives (MFAA) program** was established in 1943 under the Allied Military Government — approximately **345 officers** (many art historians, museum curators, and architects) served through the war
- Key figures: **George Stout** (conservation pioneer), **James Rorimer** (later director of the Met), **Walter Hancock**, **Lincoln Kirstein**, **Rose Valland** (French liaison)
- **Altaussee mine recovery (May 1945):** MFAA officers prevented the destruction of the mine's contents — Nazi officials had placed **eight 500-pound bombs** in the mine to destroy the art rather than let it be captured; local Austrian miners removed the bombs before the Allies arrived
- The MFAA established **Central Collecting Points** in Munich, Wiesbaden, Offenbach, and Marburg — processing hundreds of thousands of objects for return to countries of origin
- **The Offenbach Archival Depot** alone processed **3 million books and manuscripts** from looted libraries
- **Tier 1** — Military records, MFAA reports, post-war documentation

### What Was Recovered — and What Was Not

- The **vast majority** of looted art was recovered in the immediate post-war period — including the Ghent Altarpiece, Vermeer's *The Astronomer*, and Michelangelo's *Bruges Madonna*
- However, an estimated **100,000 artworks remain missing** as of 2025 — dispersed through black market sales, destruction during bombing, Soviet trophy seizures, and unresolved Cold War-era appropriations
- The **Soviet Union famously shipped "trophy art"** from Germany — including the **Priam's Treasure** (Schliemann's Troy excavation finds) and works from the **Dresden Gallery** — Russia still holds many of these objects despite international restitution claims
- **Tier 1** — Recovery statistics documented; missing-art databases maintained by international organizations

## §5 — RESTITUTION AND ONGOING CASES

### Legal Framework

- **1998 Washington Conference Principles on Nazi-Confiscated Art** — 44 nations agreed to identify looted art in public collections, establish provenance research, and seek "fair and just solutions" for restitution claims
- **2009 Terezín Declaration** — expanded commitment to restitution of cultural property, including immovable property and Judaica
- **Major restitution cases:**
  - **Klimt paintings / Maria Altmann v. Republic of Austria** (2006) — US Supreme Court; five Klimt paintings returned from the Austrian Belvedere gallery, including *Portrait of Adele Bloch-Bauer I* (later sold for $135 million)
  - **Gurlitt collection** (2012–present) — 1,500+ works found in Cornelius Gurlitt's Munich apartment and Salzburg house; task force established; works with proven provenance claims returned; remainder bequeathed to the Kunstmuseum Bern
  - **Ongoing claims** against major museums worldwide — the provenance of works acquired between 1933–1950 remains under continuous scrutiny
- **Criticism:** Restitution has been slow, inconsistent, and often favors institutional holders over claimants — many families who lost art in the Holocaust lack the resources for prolonged legal battles
- **Tier 1** — Legal proceedings are public record

## §6 — THE DESTRUCTION OF LIBRARIES AND ARCHIVES

### Beyond the 1933 Burnings

- Nazi library destruction extended far beyond the symbolic 1933 burnings:
  - **Poland:** The Warsaw Public Library was deliberately burned during the **1944 Warsaw Uprising suppression** — an estimated **16 million volumes** were destroyed across Poland during the war (Polish estimates)
  - **Soviet Union:** The German Army and SS destroyed libraries in Smolensk, Kiev, Minsk, and other cities — the **Belorussian Academy of Sciences library** lost approximately 2 million volumes
  - **Jewish community libraries:** The **YIVO Institute** library in Vilnius was partially looted (selected materials shipped to Germany for ERR processing); the remainder was slated for destruction
  - **Synagogue archives:** Torah scrolls, communal records, and religious manuscripts were systematically destroyed alongside communities
- Alfred Rosenberg simultaneously built a **counter-library** — confiscating Jewish and Masonic books to create a research institute for studying the "Jewish enemy" (the planned *Hohe Schule* was never completed)
- **Tier 1** — Wartime destruction records, post-war surveys

## §7 — COUNTER-ARGUMENTS AND CRITICISMS

### Complicating Factors

- **Western Allies also damaged cultural property:** Allied bombing destroyed significant cultural sites — the firebombing of Dresden (February 1945) damaged the Zwinger Palace and Semperoper; other bombing campaigns destroyed churches, libraries, and museums across Germany and Italy
- **Restitution asymmetries:** Soviet trophy art seizures were justified as reparations for the **27 million Soviet war dead** and massive German destruction of Soviet cultural property — the restitution debate involves legitimate competing claims
- **Market complicity:** Western art dealers, auction houses, and collectors profited from Nazi-era art sales — the art market's role in laundering looted property extended well beyond German borders
- **Selectivity of memory:** The 1933 book burnings are heavily commemorated; the **far larger destruction** of Polish, Soviet, and Jewish libraries during the war itself receives less popular attention

### Enduring Significance

- The Nazi program of cultural destruction and theft was **unprecedented in its systematic, bureaucratic nature** — not incidental to war, but a deliberate extension of genocide
- The **cultural dimension of the Holocaust** — the destruction of Judaica, synagogues, libraries, and communal records — aimed to erase not only Jewish people but Jewish **civilization** (what Raphael Lemkin, who coined the term "genocide," called "cultural genocide")
- The legal and institutional frameworks created in response (MFAA, Washington Principles, provenance research) established **foundational precedents** for cultural property protection in armed conflict
- Heinrich Heine's 1820 observation — "Where they burn books, they will ultimately burn people also" (*Dort, wo man Bücher verbrennt, verbrennt man auch am Ende Menschen*) — proved prophetic
