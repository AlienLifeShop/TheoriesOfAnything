### 1.2 The Double-Slit Experiment **[3/3+ SOURCES]**

**Reliability: TIER 1 — FOUNDATION EXPERIMENT**

| Stage | What Happens |
|-------|-------------|
| **Step 1** | Fire particles (photons, electrons, even molecules) one at a time at a barrier with two slits |
| **Step 2** | With no detector at the slits — an **interference pattern** forms on the screen behind (wave behavior; the particle goes through BOTH slits simultaneously) |
| **Step 3** | Place a detector at the slits to observe which slit the particle passes through — the interference pattern **disappears**; particles behave as particles |
| **Step 4** | The act of **observation** determines whether the particle behaves as a wave or particle |
| **Delayed choice** | Wheeler's delayed-choice experiment (1978, confirmed 2007): the decision to observe can be made AFTER the particle has already passed the slits — and it STILL affects the result |
| **Implications** | Reality appears to be **contextual** — it responds to observation. The observer is not separate from the observed. The past is not fixed until it is observed. |

### 1.3 Superposition

**Reliability: TIER 1 — EXPERIMENTALLY VERIFIED**

| Aspect | Detail |

| **Verification** | Superposition has been demonstrated with photons, electrons, atoms, and even molecules with 2,000+ atoms (Fein et al., 2019, *Nature Physics*) |
| **Scale question** | Why don't we see superposition at the macro scale? This is the "measurement problem" — still unsolved |
